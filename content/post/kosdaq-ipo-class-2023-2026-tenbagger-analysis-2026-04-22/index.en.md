---

## TL;DR

Of <strong>308 KOSDAQ new listings from 2023-2026</strong>, exactly <strong>49 are SPACs</strong> (identified by "SPAC" in the Korean ticker name). Strip those out and the real operating universe is <strong>259 firms</strong>.

From that 259:

- <strong>39 firms (15%)</strong> have returned +100% or more since IPO
- <strong>33 firms (13%)</strong> are down -50% or worse
- <strong>53% are still below the IPO price</strong>
- <strong>44% of the winners cluster in 3 sector buckets</strong> — R&D (pharma pipeline), semiconductors, medical devices. Those three buckets hold <strong>48 firms</strong>, barely 19% of the universe, but <strong>17 of the 39 tenbaggers</strong> came from them.

The single strongest numerical signal: firms with <strong>YoY revenue growth above 200%</strong> show a <strong>57% tenbagger hit rate</strong> (4 of 7). The next bucket (50–200% growth) drops to 29%. Above 200% is effectively a structural filter.

Below: the full decomposition, then an 11-name Tier 1 shortlist to move into qualitative review.

## 1. Redefining the universe: SPACs cleanly removed

The cleanest SPAC filter is the Korean ticker-name rule: any listed shell with "스팩" (SPAC) in the name. That removes 49 names across four cohorts — most heavily from the 2024 cohort (36 SPACs alone).

Names sometimes flagged as "shells" but which are <strong>genuine operating businesses</strong> and therefore kept: LB Investment, HB Investment, Capstone Partners (all three are VC fund managers with real carry revenue), Hanpass (FX remittance fintech), Terra View. These stay in.

<strong>Three observations that pay off later:</strong>

1. <strong>2024 is the paradox cohort.</strong> Median return -25.5% (worst of any year), but <strong>all four +500% winners came from 2024</strong> — Sceers Medical, D&D Pharmatech, Samhyun, HVM. Classic high-variance distribution: the median gets crushed while the right tail produces the biggest hits.
2. <strong>2025 is the "expensive IPO of mature companies" cohort.</strong> Median revenue 50.1B KRW (highest of any year — these were real companies at scale), but -48% median drawdown from peak. Pricing was rich; de-rating is mechanical. The distribution suggests <strong>12–18 months out, this cohort should throw off re-entry opportunities at de-rated multiples</strong>.
3. <strong>Operating-profit rate isn't a quality signal here.</strong> 2024 had the highest profitability (49% of listings profitable at IPO) yet the weakest post-IPO returns. High profitability at listing correlates with "mature companies priced near fair value" — exactly the opposite of where tenbaggers come from.

### Three winner buckets

<strong>Bio R&D + Semiconductors + Medical devices = 48 firms, 19% of universe, but 17 of the 39 +100% hits (44%).</strong> That concentration is not noise — it reflects a structural reality:

- <strong>Bio R&D winners</strong> compound on <strong>clinical milestones and out-licensing deals</strong>, not current revenue. So a firm can have 4.3bn KRW median revenue and still hit +312% at the p90.
- <strong>Semiconductor winners</strong> ride specific cycles: HBM-adjacent, AI-server SSD, wafer-back-end. Five listings hit +100% with median P/E of 59x — the market pays for the asymmetric call on new memory architectures.
- <strong>Medical devices</strong> have the highest profitability (50% profit rate) *and* a +260% p90. That combination is rare. Korean medical devices with FDA approval + US distribution are structurally underpriced because the local market doesn't know how to value US-scale med-tech.

### Two loser buckets

<strong>Software (36 firms) + Other chemicals (11 firms) = 47 firms, explaining most of the loser population.</strong>

- <strong>Software</strong> is the "AI premium paid at IPO" problem. 61% loser rate. Thin moats, low switching cost, and the "AI wrapper" narrative deflated as soon as enterprise buyers got sober in mid-2024.
- <strong>Other chemicals</strong> is the 2차전지 (secondary battery) peak-out. 91% loser rate (10 of 11 down). Battery-material listings rode the 2022-2023 EV mania into IPO and then met the 2024-2025 demand correction head-on.

### The middle zone

Special-purpose machinery, electronic components, general machinery — all "real revenue, boring multiples, mean-reverting performance." Median revenue 30-82bn KRW (the biggest of any bucket) but median return near zero. This is the classic Korean KOSDAQ equipment/parts segment: legitimate businesses, cyclically priced, no asymmetric upside at IPO.

## 7. What this framework actually says

| Question | Answer from the 259-firm backtest |
|---|---|
| Base rate of finding a +100% winner at KOSDAQ IPO | <strong>15%</strong> (39/259) |
| Base rate of finding a +500% winner | <strong>1.9%</strong> (5/259) |
| Worst cohort to chase | <strong>2024</strong> at listing (median -25.5%, 61% losers — though the right-tail winners came from here) |
| Best cohort to hunt in right now | <strong>2025</strong> (richest base — median revenue 50bn — currently at -48% median drawdown) |
| Single most predictive metric | <strong>YoY revenue growth ≥ 200% → 57% hit rate</strong> |
| Sectors to live in | Bio R&D, semiconductors, medical devices (44% of winners, 19% of universe) |
| Sectors to skip | Software (61% loser rate), secondary-battery chemicals (91% loser rate) |

The framework isn't a prediction engine. It's a <strong>base-rate engine</strong>. It says: given how money has actually been made in new KOSDAQ listings over the last three years, these 11 names sit on more of the right factors than any others in the cohort. Qualitative review comes next — IR material, disclosure history, consensus positioning — and position sizing follows that.

## 8. Caveats and honest uncertainties

- <strong>YoY growth percentages are noisy in bio R&D.</strong> Most have base revenues of 3-10bn KRW, so a "+7,800%" print (Algenomics) is the jump from 100M to 8bn — a milestone payment, not a business inflection. For bio names, look at <strong>absolute revenue + pipeline milestones</strong>, not YoY %.
- <strong>Fundamentals are an 8-day snapshot.</strong> The ROE/P/E here come from the 2026-04-14 through 04-21 filings window. Next quarterly reports will reshuffle some of these names.
- <strong>Green Resource, Fadu, Sonics</strong> — 3-month momentum ≥ +180%. Chasing these here carries a sharp drawdown risk. The Tier 1 placement reflects structural fit, not "buy now."
- <strong>Re-entry on past +1,000% winners.</strong> D&D Pharmatech and Sceers Medical have already delivered 10×. Another 5-10× from here requires a *new* catalyst — new market, new indication, new geography. Without it, the base case is mean reversion.
- <strong>Consensus / target-price fields are empty in our data.</strong> Sell-side positioning isn't reflected. IR contact and disclosure-level review is mandatory before sizing.
