## 2. Reading the data

<strong>[Fact] Sales rank has clearly recovered post-Patch 1.07.</strong>
The 24-hour global rank-average pre-patch was <strong>#29.0</strong>; post-patch the running average is <strong>#24.7</strong>, with the latest at <strong>#18</strong>. The US moves from a pre-patch average of <strong>#29.1</strong> to <strong>#23.7</strong>, latest <strong>#19</strong>.

<strong>[Fact] User count is still below last weekend.</strong>
Both average and peak CCU are down \~<strong>12%</strong> vs the prior weekend. This reads less as "users have rolled over" and more as "the decay rate has slowed and the weekend peak has reformed."

<strong>[Inference] The bigger change is review quality.</strong>
New-review positive rate this weekend is <strong>94.0%</strong>, sharply up from last weekend's <strong>82.1%</strong>. After Patch 1.06 there was clearly community friction and downward review pressure — Patch 1.07 appears to have eased most of it.

<strong>[Fact] Patch 1.07's content is long-tail focused.</strong>
Per the official notes, 1.07 added boss rematches, new Damiane combat skills, mount additions, and an AMD-driver crash fix. It is not a pure bug-fix patch — it strengthens repeat play, combat depth, and collection loops. Source: [Crimson Desert Patch Notes 1.07.00](https://crimsondesert.pearlabyss.com/en-US/News/Notice/Detail?_boardNo=92).

## 3. China rank divergence — a new signal

The most interesting new signal in the data is that <strong>the Steam China sales rank is consistently stronger than the global rank</strong> — and it has held for a week, not 1–2 days.

### 3.1 Global vs China average rank by window

| Window | Global avg | China avg | Gap |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Last 24h | #22.2 | <strong>#18.2</strong> | China +4.0 rungs |
| Last 48h | #26.1 | <strong>#19.3</strong> | China +6.7 rungs |
| Last 7d | #23.4 | <strong>#17.3</strong> | China +6.2 rungs |
| Post Patch 1.07 | #24.3 | <strong>#19.0</strong> | China +5.3 rungs |
| Since May 11 | #24.3 | <strong>#17.7</strong> | China +6.6 rungs |

Over the past 7 days, China was better than global on <strong>98.6%</strong> of observations — i.e., almost always.

### 3.2 Why this matters — the thesis shifts by one line

The initial thesis was "global AAA hit." If China is now stronger than global, the thesis shifts slightly.

Prior signal set:

- North-America / global-led AAA sales
- Held a Steam global Top Seller position
- Console + PC concurrent strength

Added signal set:

- <strong>Steam-China demand attaching with a lag</strong>
- China rank decays less even when global decays
- Post-patch Chinese-user response and purchase conversion are relatively stronger

In particular, when global averaged #26–30 between May 13–15, China held #18–21. Open-world combat, boss rematches, fast patch cadence, and high-spec PC play culture may simply map well to the Chinese Steam audience.

### 3.3 What it is *not*

<strong>[Blocked]</strong> Today's data cannot confirm any of:

- Formal mainland-China distribution (license / 판호)
- WeGame release
- A formal Tencent partnership
- China-mainland gross revenue scale

The Steam China rank is <strong>a relative indicator of Steam-China demand</strong>, not a measure of formal mainland sales. The figure mixes mainland VPN users, Hong Kong, Taiwan, and Chinese-language SEA users.

### 3.4 Trading read — bullish but not yet monetized

This is a <strong>"China optionality is waking up"</strong> signal. But it is not yet a price-moving catalyst. For the market to re-price, at least one of the following needs to land:

- China rank persistently above global
- Steam China rank settled inside Top 15
- Clear inflection in Chinese community / streaming / review traction
- A formal Tencent / WeGame / license / China-publishing signal
- The company explicitly addresses China traction on a print or IR call

When two or more of these stack together, Pearl Abyss gets reclassified from "package-game maker" to <strong>"global AAA IP with China optionality"</strong>. Pearl Abyss already has Tencent shareholding ties and existing China business optionality — these reads carry more weight than a generic regional-rank improvement.

## 5. Investment read

This weekend's data <strong>rebuts the bear thesis</strong>. The picture is not "sales rank slipped beyond #30 and the structure broke." If anything, recovering to global #18 and US #19 post-patch supports the "fast patches sustain sales" thesis.

But this is <strong>not a bull confirmation</strong>. CCU is still -12% vs last weekend and average sales rank is below last week's. Today's data says "decay controlled and rank recovered," not "re-acceleration."

Practical read:

- <strong>Current positions:</strong> hold
- <strong>Adding:</strong> conditional
- <strong>Conditions to add:</strong> global Top 20 settled into Sunday, US Top 25 held, weekend peak near or above 70K
- <strong>Warning signs:</strong> post-Monday slip back outside global #30, US stuck outside #35, new-review positive rate below 80%
- <strong>Thesis-kill conditions:</strong> the formal 6M announcement slips into mid-June *and* DLC / roadmap / capital-return / DokeV visibility fails to land while sales rank keeps falling

The cleanest summary is that this weekend is not "we're hanging on" — it is closer to "the patch makes it sell again." For Pearl Abyss to be re-rated as a global AAA studio with a proprietary engine, the next step set is clear: <strong>a formal 6M, DLC or a long-tail roadmap, and a capital-return signal</strong> all have to land together. If the <strong>§3 China-optionality signal</strong> holds for another 1–2 weeks, the holding thesis is reinforced one more notch — and if it disappears in the same window, it gets reclassified as noise.
