## 1. The "Automatic Reflex" Trap

### 1.1 The "NVIDIA → AI-RAN → SKT" Inference Chain

The thinking pattern that drives this trade is straightforward.

```text
1. NVIDIA earnings imminent
   ↓
2. Jensen Huang likely to say "telecom is next"
   ↓
3. AI-RAN theme gains attention
   ↓
4. "SKT is Korea's AI-RAN flagship"
   ↓
5. Buy SKT
```

The problem is that every link looks plausible. NVIDIA has genuinely talked up telecom and AI-RAN; SK Telecom has genuinely articulated an AI infrastructure and 6G vision. But whether step 4 is actually true — and whether step 5 is an efficient trade — requires separate verification.

Most thematic trades stop here. They treat "the word fits" and "the money flows here" as synonymous. They are not.

### 1.2 Why SKT Looks Attractive — The Facts

There are real reasons SKT appears to be Korea's AI-RAN proxy.

```text
✓ Member of NVIDIA's Global 6G Coalition (12 carriers)
✓ "AI Native" strategy announced at MWC 2026
✓ Supermicro Haein cluster: 1,000+ AI servers deployed
✓ Joint vRAN/AI-RAN white paper published with DOCOMO
✓ 6G standardization MOU with Ericsson through 2031
✓ Q1 2026 AIDC revenue: KRW 131.4 bn (+89% YoY)
✓ Foreign net buying past 1 week: \~KRW 87.8 bn
✓ Stock outperformed May 11–13
```

On the surface this is compelling: defensive telecom fundamentals, an AI infrastructure growth story, and membership in a global 6G network. Near-term technicals are positive too.

### 1.3 What the Market Tends to Miss

Reading the same facts differently changes the picture.

```text
✗ 6G Coalition of 12 = "coalition member" (group mention)
✗ NVIDIA field-trial carriers named directly ≠ SKT
   (T-Mobile, SoftBank, IOH are the marquee names)
✗ SKT's MWC 2026 announcement = AI Native strategy (AI-RAN was one line)
✗ Q1 2026 AIDC revenue KRW 131.4 bn = \~3.0% of consolidated revenue
✗ Foreign buying = already reflected in the May 11–13 rally
✗ Stock is up \~+101% from its 52-week low
```

In other words: the linkage is thin, and much of it is already in the price. Betting on SKT alone is a thin thesis — the theme is right, but the price and the value-accrual position are the problem.

## 3. Where AI-RAN's Economic Value Actually Flows

### 3.1 The Five-Layer Value-Accrual Structure

When one unit of AI-RAN infrastructure is deployed, spending flows through roughly five layers.

<strong>Layer 1 — GPU/HBM/Memory: the largest pool</strong>

NVIDIA GB200/GB300, L4, RTX Pro GPUs and Grace CPUs, along with HBM and DRAM inside those chips, are the heart of the system. Revenue accrues to NVIDIA and its memory supply chain — SK hynix and Samsung Electronics. This layer could absorb 40–50% of total AI-RAN CAPEX.

<strong>Layer 2 — vRAN/Network Software</strong>

5G/6G vRAN stacks, AI channel estimation, schedulers, and network optimization software live here. Samsung Networks, Nokia, and Ericsson are the key players, accounting for roughly 15–20% of CAPEX.

<strong>Layer 3 — RU/RF/Power Amplifiers</strong>

Radio units, GaN PAs, filters, antennas, and mMIMO hardware. RFHIC, KMW, and the RU divisions of Nokia and Ericsson are involved. Share: roughly 10–15%.

<strong>Layer 4 — Optical Interconnects/CPO</strong>

800G/1.6T optical transceivers, ELSFP, CPO, Spectrum-X, and ConnectX-class networking. NVIDIA on one end; Oi Solutions, Bit&Electron, and Lycom on the Korean supply side. Share: roughly 5–10%.

<strong>Layer 5 — Operators / CAPEX Spenders</strong>

SK Telecom, T-Mobile, SoftBank, and IOH sit here. Their role is to buy layers 1–4. Revenue may eventually return to them through network efficiency gains and future edge AI service monetization — but from a pure AI-RAN CAPEX perspective, they are cost centers.

This is the most important insight. <strong>Operators are not the ones receiving the money. They are the ones spending it.</strong>

### 3.2 Why Operators Trend Toward Cost Centers

Every generation — 2G, 3G, 4G, 5G — has taught the same lesson.

Operators built out new networks, delivered services, fought to grow ARPU, then repeated the whole process for the next generation standard. The parties that captured value first and most reliably were equipment vendors, chip companies, memory suppliers, and device manufacturers.

```text
Who made money each cycle:
- Equipment: Nokia, Ericsson, Samsung, Huawei
- Chips: Qualcomm, MediaTek
- Memory: Samsung, SK hynix, Micron
- Devices: Apple, Samsung

Korean telecom ROE by cycle:
- 4G cycle (2010–2015): 5–10%
- 5G cycle (2019–2024): 5–9%
- 6G cycle (2027–?): not yet established
```

Operator ROE failed to expand meaningfully through the 5G cycle. AI-RAN could change that pattern — but the evidence is not yet there. The thesis that "AI-RAN = operator re-rating" remains thin at this stage.

### 3.3 Scenarios Where Operators Capture Profit

For an operator to monetize AI-RAN meaningfully, it needs to evolve beyond being a simple network provider.

```text
1. "Network operator" → "edge AI service operator"
   - Run AI workloads on the network and charge for them
   - AI-as-a-service model
   - Convergence of connectivity and compute

2. Sovereign AI infrastructure operator
   - Data-sovereignty guarantees for governments and enterprises
   - Long-duration contracts for in-country AI infrastructure
   - New revenue category

3. B2B AI solutions integrator
   - Autonomous vehicles, smart cities, smart factories
   - Combined 5G/6G + AI solutions
   - Telecom + platform + solutions bundled
```

Moving toward these models is how an operator lifts its margins. This is roughly the direction SKT is pursuing. But it takes time to recognize these revenue streams. The fastest monetization path is AIDC + GPUaaS — that is SKT's real thesis, not AI-RAN.

## 4. Four Market Misconceptions — Addressed Directly

### 4.1 Misconception 1 — "AI-RAN theme = buy SKT"

This is an unfounded reflex.

```text
"NVIDIA mentions telecom" → "buy Korean telecom"
```

Three problems compound: auto-mapping "Korean telecom" to SKT specifically; treating the operator as the beneficiary of value accrual; and placing SKT among the global marquee field-trial carriers.

The corrected inference chain:

```text
NVIDIA mentions telecom
  ↓
Telecom CAPEX cycle accelerates
  ↓
Infrastructure component companies benefit
  ↓
Samsung Networks, RFHIC, Oi Solutions
```

SKT appears at layer 5. It is a lower-priority destination. Its position as a CAPEX spender must be kept in view.

### 4.2 Misconception 2 — "SKT is a global AI-RAN leader"

Partly right, partly wrong.

The part that is right: policy and R&D. SKT is an AI-RAN Alliance board member, is active in 6G standardization, and has clearly articulated an AI Native vision.

The part that is wrong: field implementation. T-Mobile, SoftBank, and IOH lead at the proof-of-concept level. SoftBank in particular is furthest ahead on the commercialization story.

An analogy: SKT is closer to the chair of an AI-RAN academic committee; T-Mobile and SoftBank are closer to AI-RAN's first paying customers. The chair role has value, but markets respond faster to the first customers.

### 4.3 Misconception 3 — "AIDC revenue grew 89%; time to buy"

The number looks impressive in isolation: Q1 2026 AIDC revenue of KRW 131.4 bn, +89% YoY.

But context requires checking the mix.

```text
Consolidated revenue: KRW 4,392.3 bn
AIDC revenue:         KRW 131.4 bn
AIDC share = 131.4 / 4,392.3 ≈ 3.0%
```

AIDC is growing fast — that is real. But it is 3% of the group. Its EPS impact is smaller still.

If 89% growth sustains, the picture changes: AIDC could reach roughly 10% of revenue in two years and above 20% in three. But it takes 2–3 years to reach a meaningful group-level share. As a short-term AI beta vehicle, SKT remains weak.

### 4.4 Misconception 4 — "Foreign investors are buying, so I should follow"

Foreign net buying of roughly KRW 87.8 bn over the past five sessions is a clear flow signal, with heavy buying on May 11–12 sustained through subsequent consolidation.

But May 11–13 saw a strong price move. Much of the foreign buying's price impact is already reflected; chasing it now means arriving as a late buyer.

Why foreigners are buying requires separate analysis — it could be a mix of AIDC growth, defensive telecom appeal, and advance pricing of the AI-RAN theme. Foreign buying is not a sufficient condition for chasing.

### 5.2 Name-by-Name Thesis Summary

<strong>Samsung Electronics — #1 priority</strong>

Samsung Electronics has three layers of AI-RAN exposure: HBM and DRAM that go into NVIDIA servers; Samsung Networks vRAN software; and AI-MIMO algorithms validated in a multi-cell NVIDIA-based test environment.

The appeal is cross-layer exposure to the entire AI-RAN stack, with a memory supercycle as the base case and AI-RAN as a free option. The stock also trades at roughly 5× 2027E P/E — the cheapest name in the basket on a valuation basis.

Risks include the May 21 labor strike, a secondary position to SK hynix in HBM market share, and the integrated-device-manufacturer discount. The call is to hold on the watchlist and accumulate in tranches after macro stabilizes. If the strike resolves and NVIDIA beats simultaneously, the case for adding weight strengthens.

<strong>RFHIC — #3 priority, conditional</strong>

RFHIC supplies GaN packages for RU power amplifiers and sits inside Samsung Networks' equipment supply chain, benefiting from 5G, AI-RAN, and defense simultaneously.

Q1 2026: revenue KRW 43.1 bn, operating profit KRW 7.7 bn, OPM 18% — the turnaround is confirmed. Absolute returns over the past twelve months have been very strong. For direct exposure to the RU/RF component layer in an AI-RAN ramp, RFHIC has the highest purity.

The issue is valuation: roughly 60× 2026E P/E and roughly 40× 2027E P/E means a great deal of expectation is already embedded. A better entry point would follow a new order announcement or a price pullback.

<strong>Oi Solutions — #4 priority, option</strong>

Oi Solutions is exposed to 1.6 Tbps OSFP optical transceivers for AI datacenters, ELSFP for CPO applications, and AI-RAN fronthaul/backhaul optical interconnects.

Positives: expected to turn profitable in 2026, growing datacom mix, simultaneous exposure to AI-RAN and AI datacenters. But ELSFP volume production visibility matters more from 2027 onward, and recent price volatility has been elevated. A more measured approach is to wait for Q3 2026 ELSFP sampling confirmation before entering.

<strong>KMW — #5 priority, on hold</strong>

KMW carries C-band 64TRx 640W mMIMO, antenna, filter, and RU beta. But Q1 2026 revenue was KRW 21.5 bn with an operating loss of KRW 5.7 bn — fundamental proof remains weak. With a lossmaking company trading at an elevated P/B, chasing before a quarterly profit turn is not advisable.

<strong>Small-cap optical names — #6 priority, speculative</strong>

Bit&Electron, Lycom, and similar companies unveiled 1.6T optical transceivers, 100G ER1 Bidi, and 6G fronthaul products at OFC 2026. But the gap between product introduction and volume production revenue is wide. A safer posture for long-oriented investors is to wait for confirmed order announcements before sizing in.

The common principle across all scenarios: avoid chasing immediately before the call; let the price settle for 1–2 days after the result and check macro conditions simultaneously before acting.

## 7. Frequently Asked Questions

### 7.1 "If NVIDIA names SKT on the call, can it really go +10%?"

It can. But sell-the-news risk is also elevated.

On the day: +3–8% possible. The next day: further gains or profit-taking. Five sessions later: partial giveback likely. Ten to thirty days out: actual revenue and order flow will determine direction.

So "named + chase" is a short-term volatility trade. "Named + wait one week + buy the dip" is the safer approach. And the naming itself needs to connect to real revenue to generate lasting alpha; a group mention has limited information value.

### 7.2 "Even so, isn't SKT still Korea's most direct AI-RAN name?"

Depends on how you define "direct."

From an operator/field-trial standpoint, SKT is the most engaged Korean telecom, with a clearer AI-RAN and 6G narrative than KT or LG Uplus.

From an economic-value-accrual standpoint, Samsung Electronics ranks ahead. It holds both vRAN and HBM exposure simultaneously. As the Korean partner NVIDIA has most explicitly validated in an AI-RAN context, Samsung Networks is the more direct tie.

What matters for investment is value accrual. SKT can legitimately claim the label "Korea's AI-RAN representative" — but only as an operator, not as the primary beneficiary of AI-RAN spending.

### 7.3 "Are you saying don't buy SKT at all?"

No. SKT is a solid company. The question is why you are buying it.

Good reasons: AIDC revenue growth, GPUaaS expansion, core telecom + dividend + defensive characteristics, sovereign AI infrastructure option.

Poor reasons: "NVIDIA might mention AI-RAN," "foreigners are buying," "it's an AI theme stock," "it's the most AI-sounding telecom."

Given that the stock is significantly above its 52-week low, new buyers are better served by waiting for a pullback — or for Q2 AIDC visibility to confirm the growth trajectory.

### 7.4 "How do I construct a supply-chain basket?"

It depends on your holding horizon and volatility tolerance.

<strong>Defensive</strong>: Samsung Electronics and SK hynix as the core. Lower direct AI-RAN exposure, but the safest names in the basket.

<strong>Balanced</strong>: Samsung Electronics as core with a partial allocation to Oi Solutions, RFHIC, and SK hynix. Balances direct AI-RAN exposure and stability.

<strong>Aggressive</strong>: A blend of RFHIC, Oi Solutions, Samsung Electronics, KMW, and small-cap optical names. In this case, clearing a macro gate and using tranche-based entries are essential.

The common principle: avoid over-concentrating in any single name. AI-RAN is still in early commercialization, so a basket structured around value-accrual layers is more rational than a single-name bet.

<strong>Real alpha does not start with the word "AI-RAN." It starts with asking where the economic value accrues.</strong> Ask that question precisely and the answer becomes clear: supply chain, not operator. Buying the theme label is the fastest path to losses; understanding the value-accrual architecture is the path that lasts.
