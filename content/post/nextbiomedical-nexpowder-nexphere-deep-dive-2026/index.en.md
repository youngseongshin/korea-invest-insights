## 1. Company Overview & Current Investment Interpretation

NextBiomedical should be evaluated along two business axes.

The first is <strong>Nexpowder, an endoscopic hemostatic agent</strong>. This product has already entered the commercialization phase across multiple countries including the US, Europe, and Japan, and was the core revenue driver behind most of 2025's results. It holds FDA 510(k) clearance (K202929), is distributed through Medtronic, is sold in approximately 30 countries, and demonstrated a 94% immediate hemostasis rate in a landmark GUT journal study.

The second is <strong>the Nexphere/Nexphere-F absorbable embolization microsphere platform</strong>. This axis is not yet at the large-scale revenue stage but is proving its future value through US clinical trials, Japanese regulatory approvals, and global partnerships. It has been granted FDA Breakthrough Device Designation, and first patient enrollment in the RESORB pivotal trial occurred in October 2025.

The current share price simultaneously reflects both axes. Nexpowder supports the earnings floor, while Nexphere-F elevates the valuation multiple. Therefore, this company should not be viewed as a pure earnings play, nor purely as a clinical momentum play. It must be understood as a combination of <strong>a product already in commercialization + a pipeline with open-ended value</strong>.

2025 was a year where product economics improved, but the company also spent more aggressively to build its future platform.

## 6. Nexpowder (Hemostatic Agent) Progress & Milestones

### 6.1 Current Stage

Nexpowder is already in the commercialization phase:

* US FDA 510(k) clearance (K202929)
* 2024 indication expansion to lower GI bleeding
* Japan PMDA approval and insurance listing completed
* September 2025 official Japan launch
* Sold in \~30 countries
* US distribution through Medtronic
* GUT journal landmark study: 94% immediate hemostasis rate

The current bottleneck for Nexpowder is not regulatory approval. The bottleneck is <strong>adoption rate</strong>.

### 6.2 Clinical Positioning

The randomized trial published in GUT suggests Nexpowder goes beyond a simple rescue device to demonstrate <strong>re-bleeding prevention effects</strong>. This differentiates it from competitors including Cook Medical's Hemospray, EndoClot, and PuraStat.

The real upside for Nexpowder is not being another hemostatic device but rather:

> <strong>Perception shift toward a standard-of-care adjunctive treatment</strong>

### 6.3 Upcoming Milestones

1. Post-Japan-launch revenue ramp-up confirmation
2. US lower GI indication expansion reflected in earnings
3. Guideline/standard-of-care inclusion

Nexpowder has transitioned from a "regulatory approval story" to an <strong>adoption expansion story</strong>.

## 12. Conclusion

### Key Takeaways

NextBiomedical has a genuinely interesting dual structure:

* Nexpowder is already commercialized, <strong>supporting the earnings floor</strong>.
* Nexphere-F is <strong>opening the multiple ceiling</strong> through US/Japan/global partnerships.

The three most important questions right now:

1. <strong>Does Nexpowder actually re-accelerate growth in 2026?</strong>
2. <strong>Does the Nexphere-F US clinical trial stay on schedule?</strong>
3. <strong>Can revenue growth offset rising costs?</strong>

Current assessment:

* <strong>Q1 2026 is a defensive quarter</strong> — not a breakout.
* <strong>Q2 2026 onward is what matters more.</strong>
* <strong>Nexphere-F value reflection is possible, but the higher the target, the less evidence currently supports it.</strong>

### Investment Verdict

> <strong>Wait / Watchlist</strong>

This is clearly a quality company. But for the stock to meaningfully re-rate from here, it needs not more expectations but <strong>execution evidence</strong> — clinical trial progress, partnership monetization, and cost discipline.
