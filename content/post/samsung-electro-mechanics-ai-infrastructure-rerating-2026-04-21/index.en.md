## 3. Non-specialist read

Samsung Electro-Mechanics is one of the very few companies that supplies <strong>both the skeleton (FC-BGA substrate) and the blood-pressure regulator (MLCC) of an AI chip</strong>. If Nvidia's GPU is the brain, the AI server cannot function without the substrate that carries it and the MLCCs that feed stable power to it.

Until recently SEMCO was seen as a Samsung proxy — PC CPU substrates plus flagship-phone MLCC meant earnings tracked Galaxy volumes. Between 2024 and 2026 the <strong>customer mix shifted structurally</strong>: PC to AI server/data center, smartphone to AI server and automotive, and single-customer dependence to diversification across Nvidia, AMD, Tesla, Broadcom, Apple, and AWS. At the same time supply is not keeping up with demand — CEO Jang himself said demand exceeds capacity by 50%+ — so <strong>pricing power is migrating from buyers to SEMCO</strong>. The stock is pricing that transition.

## 5. Origin Technology and Technical Moat

### Core IP stack

| Area | Core tech | Difficulty / specificity |
|---|---|---|
| <strong>MLCC</strong> | Ultra-thin multilayer (hundreds of layers), BaTiO₃ particle grading and purity, 1kV+ high-voltage parts | Material and process know-how plus mass-production yield |
| <strong>FC-BGA</strong> | Fine-line patterning, large area (140×140mm+), 20+ layers, low-loss CCL materials | [Fact] Very small global group of high-end server-substrate volume producers |
| <strong>Glass substrate</strong> | In-house glass core (Sumitomo JV), TGV pitch, CTE control | [Fact] Warpage reduced -30% at room temperature and -90% at high temperature vs. organic |
| <strong>Camera modules</strong> | Folded-zoom actuators, hybrid lenses, automotive-grade modules | — |

### Moat mechanics

1. <strong>Performance</strong>: On the capacitance/voltage curve MLCC is a Murata-SEMCO duopoly. For 1kV+ AI-server-grade parts there is effectively no alternative outside these two.
2. <strong>Yield / cost</strong>: Large server FC-BGA (4× area, 2× layers) has catastrophic yield if warpage control fails. Accumulated R&D plus dedicated Busan and Vietnam lines (\~KRW 1.9T cumulative CAPEX plus another KRW 1.8T in Vietnam) create a cost-and-time barrier to followers.
3. <strong>Qualification</strong>: Big-6 supplier references (Nvidia, AMD, Tesla, Broadcom, Apple, AWS) form an infinite loop for new entrants — no qualification without references, no references without qualification. [Inferred] DB Securities cites this as the basis for "visible steady growth."
4. <strong>Ecosystem lock-in</strong>: The glass-substrate JV (with Sumitomo and Dongwoo Fine-Chem) internalizes the glass core, a potential cost-advantage lever vs. SKC's Absolics. [Fact] SEMCO holds majority of the JV.

### Hard-to-copy vs. copyable

| | Details |
|---|---|
| <strong>Moat</strong> | ① High-voltage, high-capacity MLCC for AI servers (decades of materials-process compounding); ② Server FC-BGA large-area, high-layer yield (equipment + experience compound barrier); ③ Big-Tech reference book. |
| <strong>Catch-up possible</strong> | ① PC-grade FC-BGA (Taiwan Unimicron, Japan Ibiden competitive); ② commodity IT MLCC (Taiwan Yageo, Korean Samwha); ③ camera modules (LG Innotek, Sunny Optical). |

## 9. Risks & Watchlist

### Technology risks
1. <strong>Glass-substrate reliability</strong>: Brittleness, micro-defect, seware phenomenon unresolved. Production delay would compress the valuation premium.
2. <strong>FC-BGA large-area yield</strong>: Warpage and yield on 140×140mm+ can regress. Absolute yield gap vs. Ibiden not publicly resolvable [Unclear].

### Business / customer risks
3. <strong>Rising Nvidia-platform dependence</strong>: NV Switch + Groq3 concentrate single-customer exposure. Vera Rubin shipment slippage is a direct hit.
4. <strong>Chinese MLCC pursuit + capex load</strong>: Vietnam KRW 1.8T + Sejong glass substrate capex stacked → FCF pressure. If end-market peaks, depreciation becomes a headwind.

5. <strong>US-China semi trade escalation</strong>: Direct hit to SEMCO is limited, but if Nvidia China-export restrictions tighten, GPU shipment adjustments translate indirectly to FC-BGA volumes.

## Bottom line

<strong>The three-month double is not simply an "AI-tailwind beneficiary" tag.</strong> It reflects three structural confirmations landing together:

1. <strong>Pricing-power transition</strong>: SEMCO from price-taker to price-setter (MLCC oligopoly + FC-BGA ≥50% supply shortfall).
2. <strong>Customer-mix quality step-up</strong>: Samsung dependence from 60%+ to diversified Big-6 (Nvidia, AMD, Tesla, Broadcom, Apple, AWS), and specifically a <strong>Tier-1 slot inside the Nvidia platform</strong> as a reference asset.
3. <strong>Next-gen option secured</strong>: glass-substrate JV positions SEMCO alongside SKC Absolics as the only Korean supply options.

<strong>Caveat</strong>: at \~2.8× PBR and \~18× PER (on 2025), valuation is not yet at cyclical-peak extremes, but <strong>2026E OP of KRW 1.1–1.3T is already in consensus</strong>. If earnings surprise underwhelms, the drawdown can widen. <strong>1Q26 results and the pace of ASP pass-through are the key pivot.</strong>

From a Jevons-paradox lens, AI-inference efficiency gains (TurboQuant, DeepSeek, etc.) are more likely to redirect into <strong>aggregate server-deployment growth</strong> than to depress MLCC / FC-BGA demand. At the part-and-substrate layer the mid-term demand curve should stay upward-sloping — a leverage vector that works opposite to the software-efficiency theme.
