## TL;DR

Shorts dumped 64,362 shares and still couldn't hold the close below the day's short-average price. The average short price printed KRW <strong>54,245</strong>; Pearl Abyss closed at KRW <strong>54,400</strong>. Every new short opened today is underwater at the close.

Foreigners (+28,741) and institutions (+7,960) bought the tape — net <strong>+36,701 shares</strong> absorbed against the short flow. The day ran low-to-close <strong>53,300 → 54,400</strong>, a +2.06% recovery off the low. In one sentence: they sold it, and it didn't break.

The flow story is more important than the one day. Short volume peaked on <strong>April 16 at 298,379 shares</strong> and has collapsed since: 100,764 → 86,097 → <strong>64,362</strong>. <strong>Down -78.4%</strong> from the peak in four sessions. Shorts keep showing up — but the harder they press, the less it moves.

The key is that <strong>4/16 was the short peak</strong>. The sequence since:

```

```
