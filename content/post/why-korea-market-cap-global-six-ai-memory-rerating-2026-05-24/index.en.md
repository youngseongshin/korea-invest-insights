## TL;DR

* The direction is real. WFE data show KRX domestic market capitalization at <strong>$2.67T</strong> in December 2025, up <strong>71.4% in USD terms</strong> versus the prior year. CEIC shows Korea at <strong>$4.03T</strong> in February 2026.
* The Wikipedia-style <strong>$4.89T / global No. 6 / 190% of GDP</strong> snapshot is plausible as a recent market snapshot, but the GDP ratio needs methodology checking before it should be treated as a hard input.
* Our Research OS local DB proxy puts KRX-listed market capitalization at roughly <strong>KRW 6,851T</strong> on May 22, 2026. Samsung Electronics and SK hynix together account for roughly <strong>KRW 3,093T</strong>, or <strong>45.1%</strong> of that proxy.
* The investment conclusion is not “buy Korea indiscriminately.” It is: <strong>verify AI-memory earnings durability, then look for the next bottleneck where the re-rating can broaden.</strong>

## 1. Start With the Data

Different sources use different scopes: WFE and CEIC focus on domestic listed companies; local KRX reports often include KOSPI, KOSDAQ and KONEX in won terms; public ranking tables may combine multiple sources.

| Item | Value | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---|
| WFE KRX domestic market cap, Dec. 2025 | <strong>$2.67T</strong> | +71.4% YoY in USD terms |
| CEIC Korea market cap, Feb. 2026 | <strong>$4.03T</strong> | Monthly all-time high in CEIC’s series |
| KRX-reported Korean market cap, Apr. 27, 2026 | <strong>KRW 6,031.97T</strong> | First break above KRW 6,000T |
| Public ranking table | <strong>$4.89T</strong> | Global No. 6, behind India and ahead of Taiwan |
| KOSPI close, May 22, 2026 | <strong>7,847.71</strong> | Historic high zone |
| Research OS local DB proxy, May 22, 2026 | <strong>KRW 6,851T</strong> | Proxy derived from foreign-held shares and ownership ratio |

The $4.89T snapshot is not absurd. It is about <strong>83.2%</strong> above the WFE December 2025 number and about <strong>21.4%</strong> above the CEIC February 2026 number. Given the strength of Korean equities in March-May, the direction is credible.

## 2. Be Careful With the GDP Ratio

The public table shows Korea at <strong>190% of GDP</strong>. That number implies a GDP denominator of roughly <strong>$2.57T</strong>.

CEIC, however, puts Korea’s market-cap-to-GDP ratio at <strong>149.6%</strong> for year-end 2025. It can certainly move higher in 2026 as prices rise, but the 190% figure should not be used without checking the market-cap scope, GDP denominator, FX rate and date.

Use the hierarchy this way:

* <strong>Fact:</strong> Korea market cap rose sharply from WFE’s $2.67T in Dec. 2025 to CEIC’s $4.03T in Feb. 2026.
* <strong>Likely:</strong> A late-May snapshot around the high-$4T range is directionally consistent.
* <strong>Blocked:</strong> 190% of GDP requires source-methodology validation.

## 3. The Real Driver Is AI Memory

Korea’s market-cap expansion is mostly an AI-memory story. Samsung Electronics and SK hynix are the core.

| Variable | Change | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Price | HBM and DRAM pricing power | Higher revenue per bit |
| Quantity | AI server, GPU and custom ASIC capex | More HBM, DDR5, LPDDR and SOCAMM demand |
| Cost | Fixed-cost operating leverage | Price gains flow hard into operating profit |

Our local proxy shows Samsung Electronics plus SK hynix at <strong>KRW 3,093T</strong>, or <strong>45.1%</strong> of the covered market-cap proxy. That is the heart of the story.

So yes, Korea has re-rated. But no, this is not yet a balanced full-market re-rating. It is still a concentrated large-cap semiconductor rally.

## 4. Global No. 6 Is Not a Buy Button

Market-cap rankings are late indicators. By the time a country enters the global top-six table, the first easy money has usually been made.

The headline hides three issues:

1. <strong>It is backward-looking.</strong> Rankings change after prices move.
2. <strong>It hides concentration.</strong> Korea looks broad, but Samsung and SK hynix dominate the incremental value.
3. <strong>It creates overvaluation debates.</strong> A high market-cap-to-GDP ratio can mean structural re-rating, but it can also mean a crowded position near peak earnings.

The correct response to “Korea is global No. 6” is not automatic buying. It is opening the checklist.

## 5. The Checklist

The next phase depends on five questions:

1. Are Samsung Electronics and SK hynix 2026-2027 EPS estimates still being revised up?
2. Are HBM and DRAM contract prices still firm?
3. Are foreign flows spreading beyond mega-cap semiconductors?
4. Is KOSDAQ relative strength improving for multiple sessions?
5. Is Value-Up turning into real dividends, buyback cancellation and ROE improvement?

If these hold, global No. 6 can become the middle stage of a structural Korea re-rating.

If they break, the headline becomes a late-cycle warning light.

## Conclusion

Korea’s market-cap surge is real. WFE, CEIC, KRX reports and our local Research OS proxy all point in the same direction. Korea is no longer a quiet cheap market. AI memory, Value-Up reform and global capital rotation have moved it up the global ranking table.

But the investment conclusion is colder:

<strong>Global No. 6 is not the reason to buy. The reason to buy is whether the forces that got Korea there can persist and broaden.</strong>

The next alpha is not broad Korea beta. It is the next bottleneck: AI memory, package substrates, testing, power infrastructure, silicon capacitors, and select KOSDAQ names that can turn the Korea re-rating into earnings.
