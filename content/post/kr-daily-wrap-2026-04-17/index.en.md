## Market Wrap

*Note: Most recent KR close briefing available is dated 2026-04-10; macro indicators are current as of Apr 17.*

The most recent Korea session read as a **selective risk-on day** — not a broad-market lift, but a compression rally where strong sectors grew stronger and the rest stalled or gave back intraday gains. The morning macro regime printed `KR Neutral`, yet the actual close showed a notch above that, driven by concentrated flows into three dominant themes.

**Semiconductors and AI hardware** held the top pillar. Telecom infrastructure names — including optical fiber component makers and RF component suppliers — recorded the sharpest intraday moves and drew the clearest repetition in institutional flow data. The AI-hardware-plus-telecoms-capex narrative is no longer just a single-day event; it appears to be a multi-day accumulation story as AI build-out spending ripples into Korean supply chains.

**Construction and Middle East reconstruction** re-emerged as a secondary bid. Brokerage upgrades referenced 2Q sentiment improvement and Gulf reconstruction order pipelines, lifting names like 대우건설 (047040.KS). However, the quality of that move was mixed: a meaningful number of construction names failed to hold their volume-weighted average prices intraday, suggesting the theme is real but still prone to headline-driven chasing rather than systematic accumulation. The news is good; the entry timing is not clean.

**Power and energy** (SMP prices reportedly up ~47%, ESS procurement expectations) earned structural attention but lacked direct flow confirmation into leading names, leaving the theme in a watch-and-confirm posture rather than actionable.

**Biotech** was the clear underperformer. Multiple flow signals pointed to continued rotation *out* of biotech and *into* IT hardware and semiconductor materials, a pattern consistent with a late-FTD bull cycle where leadership narrows around the highest-RS names.

The broader takeaway: **Korea's stock market character right now is compressive, not expansive**. The regime staying Neutral while KOSDAQ posts a 5-day +5.4% move tells you money is moving fast but not wide. Foreign and institutional flows are concentrating around verified leaders, not recycling into laggards. For international investors watching the KOSPI and KOSDAQ levels, the headline indices look healthy — the risk is assuming that breadth is as strong as the index moves imply.
