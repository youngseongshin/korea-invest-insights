## 1. What Haesung DS actually is — for the foreign allocator who hasn't seen this name before

Haesung DS is **not a chipmaker**. It is not a foundry, not a memory company, not an OSAT. It manufactures the **metal and substrate parts that sit inside the semiconductor package** — the lead frame that connects the die to the PCB, and the memory package substrate that connects DRAM dice to the motherboard.

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Company | Haesung DS / HAESUNG DS |
| Ticker | KOSPI 195870 |
| HQ / Sites | Seoul Gangnam HQ + Changwon plant + China / Japan / Philippines sales-production presence |
| Business segments | Lead frame + Memory package substrate + Tape substrate + Graphene |
| Reference date | 2026-05-07 |
| Closing price / market cap | KRW 86,000 / \~KRW 1.46 trillion |
| Industry positioning | **Global lead-frame #2** (Mitsui High-Tec is #1) |

The two revenue pillars are Lead Frame and Package Substrate. FY25 revenue was ₩653.4bn — Lead Frame \~₩493.8bn (75.6%) and Package Substrate \~₩159.6bn (24.4%) per Kyobo Securities' segment estimate (the company itself reports consolidated revenue and OP, not full segment splits).

For the allocator who has not previously held this name, the two-line takeaway:

* **Lead Frame is the "metal skeleton" inside a chip package**: the structure that supports the die, routes signals out to the PCB, carries current in, and dissipates heat. Automotive lead frames in particular are difficult to substitute on price because automotive ICs require long-cycle qualification, traceability, AEC-Q100 reliability data, and very low PPM defect rates. This is the first moat layer.
* **Package Substrate is a small circuit board between memory die and motherboard**. Haesung DS's edge is the **Reel-to-Reel (R2R) continuous lamination process** — closer to roll-to-roll volume manufacturing than panel-by-panel. R2R does not compete with high-layer-count AI FC-BGA (that field belongs to Samsung Electro-Mechanics, Daeduck, Ibiden, AT&S etc.), but it has cost-and-yield advantages on volume DDR4/DDR5 memory substrate.

The new optional layer being priced in at the 86,000 won close is the **AI data-center heat spreader / heat slug** opportunity sitting on top of these two pillars.

## 2. The 1Q26 print — strong revenue, broken margin

### 2-1. Quarterly P&L flow

(KRW bn)

| Period | Revenue | GP margin | Operating profit | OPM | Δ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| FY24 | 603.0 | 18.5% | 56.9 | 9.4% | base |
| FY25 | 653.4 | 15.0% | 46.5 | 7.1% | rev +8.4% / OP -18.3% |
| 4Q25 | 179.9 | 19.7% | 21.8 | 12.1% | OP QoQ +35% |
| **1Q26** | **188.7** | **12.3%** | **11.0** | **5.8%** | **rev +37.2% YoY / OP QoQ -49.6%** |
| 1Q26 TTM | 704.6 | 15.3% | 57.1 | 8.1% | rev +7.8% / OP +22.8% vs. FY25 |

The shock is sharp. Top-line print was near-record — automotive segment alone delivered \~₩91.0bn (≈48% of total). But OP halved sequentially, and OPM dropped from 12.1% in 4Q25 to 5.8% in 1Q26.

### 2-2. The cause is mechanical, not structural — but verification matters

Cu, Au, Ag, and Pd input prices rose ahead of customer ASP pass-through, with a roughly one-quarter lag. 1Q26 closed with expensive raw materials still in COGS and ASPs not yet repriced. This is the textbook "cost-pull margin" pattern in a mid-cap component company.

Three readings, in order of probability:

1. **Timing issue (high probability)** — 2Q26 ASPs catch up, OPM normalizes to \~10%. 1Q is a one-off. This is what consensus is pricing.
2. **Structural margin pressure (medium probability)** — customers resist price hikes, or low-cost Chinese LF entrants close the spread. OPM stalls at 7–8%.
3. **Demand softening (low probability)** — 2Q top line itself rolls over. The 1Q +37.2% YoY pace makes this the lowest-probability scenario right now, but EV / auto-IC inventory swings are not impossible.

Sell-side consensus (Meritz) assumes 2Q26 revenue ₩208.5bn, OP ₩20.8bn, OPM \~10%. **The 86,000 won close is priced for scenario 1.** If 2Q OP comes in below ₩15bn, the recovery thesis breaks and price retracement is rational.

## 3. Splitting the business into "core" vs. "optional" axes

The cleanest mental model for Haesung DS — and the one that makes the asymmetry visible — is to separate what is already happening from what is being priced as optionality.

### Axis 1 — Core: recovery already underway

| Item | 1Q26 status | 2026 path |
|---|---|---|
| Automotive lead frame | \~₩91bn quarterly (48% of total, near-record) | EV / auto-IC demand recovery → quarterly \~₩90bn run-rate stabilization |
| Package substrate utilization | 1H25 \~30% → 1Q26 recovery underway | KB Securities models 70%+ in 2026 → fixed-cost leverage |
| DDR5 / D1b / D1y substrate | Anonymous Korean / Chinese / US customers in qualification or early ramp | 4Q25 was the first profitable substrate quarter in seven; 2026 mix shift up |

This axis alone supports 2026 revenue of ₩733–784bn and OP of ₩79–109bn (sell-side range: iM ₩733.5bn / ₩79.0bn / 10.8% OPM, conservative; KB ₩784.3bn / ₩108.9bn / 13.9% OPM, aggressive). KB's bull case turns on substrate utilization reaching 70%.

### Axis 2 — Optional: AI data-center heat-spreader entry

This is the axis the +10.3% reaction on May 7 was about.

* The IC package heat-spreader space is \~85% concentrated in **Shinko (Japan), Honeywell/Solstice (US, post the Honeywell Advanced Materials spin-off in October 2025), Jentech (Taiwan), I-Chiun (Taiwan)**. Shinko is the long-standing server CPU heat-spreader incumbent — stamping plus surface finish is its core capability stack.
* AI accelerator power has crossed 700W+ (Blackwell, MI300 class) and is rising. Heat spreader thermal demand is climbing with package size, warpage, and TIM contact requirements. **Shinko's capacity is reportedly stretched**, and big-tech buyers are explicitly soliciting development from lead-frame suppliers as second-source candidates.
* Haesung DS's lead-frame manufacturing stack — etching, stamping, Ag plating, PPF plating, down-set, ±2μm/1m tooling, deep down-set, AEC-Q100 Grade 0 surface treatment — is the **necessary** starting point. The question is whether it is **sufficient**.
* Reporting (EBN, Samsung Securities' Lim Eun-young commentary) indicates Haesung DS is conducting heat-slug quality testing with a customer in 2Q26 with 2H26 revenue contribution if the order is confirmed.

This axis is **not yet base case for the P&L**. But it is **already partially priced in the equity**.

## 4. Mapping where Haesung DS can — and cannot — go in AI thermal

### 4-1. Four-tier AI-server thermal value chain

| Tier | Component | Key competitors | Haesung DS penetration probability |
|---|---|---|---:|
| **Package metal lid / heat spreader** | Heat slug, heat spreader, lid, stiffener | Shinko, Honeywell/Solstice, Jentech, I-Chiun | **Medium** |
| Advanced lid | Vapor chamber lid, micro-channel lid | Jentech, I-Chiun | Low |
| TIM | TIM1/1.5/2, graphite film, liquid metal, indium | Solstice, Henkel, Indium Corp | Low |
| System cooling | Cold plate, liquid cooling module, CDU, manifold | Delta, AVC, Auras, Jentech, Vertiv | Very low |

**Only tier 1 is in scope.** The investment thesis is that Haesung DS becomes a **package-level metal heat-spreader second-source**, not that it leapfrogs into liquid cooling or TIM materials.

### 4-2. Penetration probability by product difficulty

| Product | Difficulty | Penetration probability | Rationale |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Generic CPU/GPU heat spreader | Medium | **Medium–High** | Strong overlap with existing lead-frame process |
| AI ASIC / edge-AI heat slug | Medium-High | **Medium** | Customer co-design needed; easier than top-bin GPU |
| Blackwell / MI300-class large AI accelerator lid | High | **Low–Medium** | Incumbent qualification + warpage / TIM optimization barrier |
| Vapor chamber lid | Very high | **Low** | Jentech / I-Chiun ahead |
| Micro-channel lid | Very high | **Very low** | Requires fluidics / liquid-cooling integration |
| TIM materials | High | **Very low** | Solstice / Honeywell domain |
| Cold plate / liquid cooling system | High | **Very low** | Outside current business |

**The realistic 2026 read**: "AI thermal entry" should be interpreted as a **flat heat-slug second-source**, not a top-bin GPU lid contract.

### 4-3. Why the AI lid is not just a "bigger lead frame"

Lead-frame skill is necessary but not sufficient. AI package lids add requirements that aren't on the automotive LF spec sheet:

| Requirement | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Flatness / warpage control | Die-to-TIM contact failure spikes thermal resistance |
| Surface roughness / plating uniformity | Determines TIM1/TIM2 contact quality |
| CTE-mismatch management | Silicon, substrate, copper-lid thermal expansion differences create package stress |
| Outgassing / contamination control | Affects high-end package assembly yield |
| Large-area machining yield | AI packages are big — small deformation is critical |
| Customer co-design | Simultaneous optimization with Nvidia / AMD / ASIC vendor / foundry / OSAT / TIM vendor |

Flatness, plating uniformity, and surface treatment reliability are areas Haesung DS already demonstrates in automotive lead frames. CTE-mismatch management, large-area yield, and co-design with hyperscaler ASIC teams are AI-specific territory where **incumbent qualification is a binary outcome**.

**Direct revenue is not P&L-changing in 2026.** ₩29.7bn at 3% share is \~4.5% of FY25 revenue ₩653.4bn. The investment value is in **multiple compression**, not contribution margin.

### 5-2. Three scenarios

| Scenario | Product / customer | Timing | 2026 contribution | 2027 contribution | Investment implication |
|---|---|---|---:|---:|---|
| **Base** | Flat heat slug / heat spreader, anonymous big-tech or package company second-source | 2Q26 qualification → 2H26 small revenue | ₩5–20bn | ₩20–50bn | "Confirmed AI-supply-chain entry" → discount compresses |
| **Bull** | AI ASIC / GPU heat-spreader second-source, ≥2 customer qualifications | 2026 end → 2027 volume | ₩10–30bn | ₩50–100bn | Re-classified from lead-frame to AI-thermal supplier — meaningful multiple re-rating |

Heat slugs **look** like simple metal parts. They are actually package reliability components. Failing customer qualification = no revenue. The bear case is non-trivial.

## 9. Risks

### Technology
* **R2R substrate is structurally not for high-layer FC-BGA / 2.5D packaging.** Reading Haesung DS as a direct beneficiary of the AI-packaging trend is overreach.
* **AI thermal qualification is binary.** Heat slug / spreader is attractive optionality, but customer / size / margin are not yet publicly verified.

### Business / customer
* **Customer concentration is opaque.** Per-customer revenue isn't disclosed; quantifying memory-customer or auto-IDM dependence is hard.
* **Auto exposure \~75–77%.** EV / autonomous-driving slowdown hits directly. Memory mix is the lowest in the four-name peer cluster — the direct memory super-cycle benefit is the smallest.
* **Raw-material pass-through lag.** Cu / Au / Ag / Pd spikes can replay 1Q26-type margin damage.

### Macro / regulatory
* **EV demand and policy volatility** drive the auto LF axis.
* **US-China supply chain rebalancing** affects the global customer mix, export controls, and FX.

### Valuation
* **The discount is double-edged.** Cheap-for-a-reason is partially structural (auto weight + substrate latecomer) and may not compress in a single quarter.

## 10. FAQ

**Q: Is Haesung DS a clean HBM proxy?**
A: No. Haesung DS does not produce HBM stacks. AI infrastructure expansion lifts DDR4 / DDR5 substrate demand, which is a **second-order beneficiary** position. For direct HBM exposure, see the [SK Hynix HBM market-share analysis](/post/sk-hynix-hbm-market-share-ai-memory-demand-2026/).

**Q: Can Haesung DS displace Shinko?**
A: Not in the near term. Shinko holds incumbent qualification on server CPU heat spreaders. The realistic outcome is **coexistence as second-source** — capacity gap absorption, not replacement. Big-tech buyers want supply diversification; Haesung DS fits that allocator demand, not the disruption narrative.

**Q: Is 1Q26 OPM 5.8% structural?**
A: Probably not. The +37.2% YoY top line and the documented one-quarter raw-material pass-through lag both point to a timing issue. **The 2Q26 OPM print is the verification gate** — recovery to 10%+ resolves the question; persistence at ≤7% would force a structural re-read.

**Q: Isn't 75% automotive exposure too concentrated?**
A: This is the principal two-sided risk. Strong EV / auto-IC demand carries axis 1; auto-cycle softness hits directly. The thesis depends on axis 2 (DDR5 substrate utilization) and axis 3 (heat spreader) accumulating to **dilute the auto dependency**. As a single-axis name, this is risky; as a three-axis re-rating candidate, it is viable.

**Q: Is the 86,000 close chaseable?**
A: Not for a full-size book. The asymmetric entry is at the 82,000–84,000 retest. Chase only as a 30–50bp pilot. 76,000 break = re-evaluate the technical structure. Allocator translation: "good name, wrong price."

**Q: How does this fit alongside other Korean AI substrate names?**
A: It occupies a slot none of the other ten cluster names hold. Samsung Electro-Mechanics, Daeduck, Isu Petasys, Simmtech, Korea Circuit, Doosan Electro-BG, Kolon, Pamicell, TLB, and Taesung are all on the substrate / CCL / equipment axis. Haesung DS is the **only "lead-frame → AI thermal"** path. From a cluster-construction viewpoint, this adds genuine angle diversification rather than another correlated AI-substrate proxy.

**Q: What if axis 3 (heat spreader) fails entirely?**
A: Axis 1 (auto LF recovery) + axis 2 (DDR5 substrate utilization) alone supports 2026 OP ₩70–90bn and cap/OP \~16–21× — a defensible stand-alone case. The 86,000 close has partially priced in axis 3, so failure could send the stock back toward \~70,000 — but the company itself does not require axis 3 to be solvent.

**Q: When is the cleanest moment to add?**
A: Three combinations make the thesis "confirmed" rather than "priced":

1. **2Q26 OPM ≥10%** + heat-slug qualification mention,
2. **Disclosed CAPEX or line conversion** in 2Q–3Q26,
3. **A new revenue line item in 3Q26.**

Two of those three converging is sufficient for a position upgrade from "watchlist / pilot" to "core."
