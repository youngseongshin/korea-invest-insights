> This is a follow-up to [Why Korea Part 5: Korea’s Stock Market Is Now Near Global No. 6](/post/why-korea-market-cap-global-six-ai-memory-rerating-2026-05-24/). That post asked why Korea’s market cap has exploded. This one asks a more practical question: <strong>who was buying, and who was selling?</strong>

## TL;DR

* Foreign investors did not simply abandon Korea. They sharply reduced <strong>Samsung Electronics and SK hynix</strong>, the two AI-memory mega-caps.
* Research OS local DB shows foreign net selling of <strong>KRW 89.2T</strong> across 2,730 Korean stocks from Jan. 2 to May 22, 2026. Samsung Electronics contributed <strong>KRW -50.4T</strong> and SK hynix <strong>KRW -34.4T</strong>. Together: <strong>KRW -84.8T</strong>, or roughly 95% of total net selling.
* Samsung Electronics rose <strong>127.6% YTD</strong> while foreigners sold <strong>KRW 50.4T</strong> and foreign ownership fell from <strong>52.37% to 48.39%</strong>.
* SK hynix rose <strong>186.7% YTD</strong> while foreigners sold <strong>KRW 34.4T</strong>. This looks less like thesis abandonment and more like distribution after an extreme HBM-led rally.
* The portfolio conclusion is: <strong>hold core memory exposure, avoid chasing, and watch whether foreign buying rotates into non-memory sectors with real earnings.</strong>

## 1. The Main Decomposition

| Segment | Foreign net buying |
|---|---:|
| Korea total, 2,730 stocks | <strong>KRW -89.2T</strong> |
| Samsung Electronics | <strong>KRW -50.4T</strong> |
| SK hynix | <strong>KRW -34.4T</strong> |
| Ex-Samsung Electronics | KRW -38.8T |
| Ex-Samsung Electronics + SK hynix | <strong>KRW -4.4T</strong> |

The key line is the last one. Excluding Samsung Electronics and SK hynix, the foreign outflow shrinks to <strong>KRW -4.4T</strong>. The better description is therefore:

> Foreign investors were not primarily selling Korea. They were cutting Korea’s AI-memory mega-cap exposure.

Seoul Economic Daily similarly reported that from May 7 to May 22, SK hynix and Samsung Electronics accounted for more than 83% of foreign net selling on the KOSPI. ([Seoul Economic Daily](https://en.sedaily.com/finance/2026/05/23/foreign-investors-dump-46-trillion-won-targeting-samsung))

## 2. Samsung Electronics: Strong Price, Foreign Distribution

| Item | Value |
|---|---:|
| Period | 2026-01-02 to 2026-05-22 |
| Price | KRW 128,500 → KRW 292,500 |
| YTD return | <strong>+127.6%</strong> |
| Intraday high | KRW 300,500, May 22 |
| Foreign net buying | <strong>KRW -50.4T</strong> |
| Institutional net buying | KRW +6.3T |
| Retail net buying | KRW +29.2T |
| Foreign ownership | 52.37% → 48.39% |

Samsung Electronics remains a core Korean equity. But this was not a foreign-led rally. Foreigners sold through most of the move, while domestic liquidity absorbed the supply.

The most important interval was <strong>May 7-May 22</strong>. Samsung rose another <strong>7.7%</strong>, while foreigners sold <strong>KRW 18.9T</strong>. That is a sign of a strong market, but also a sign of heavy distribution near the top. Yonhap also reported that on May 7 Samsung reached the “270,000 won” level despite large foreign selling in both Samsung Electronics and SK hynix. ([Yonhap](https://www.yna.co.kr/view/AKR20260507045751008))

## 3. SK hynix: Even Stronger HBM Leadership, Same Distribution Pattern

| Item | Value |
|---|---:|
| Period | 2026-01-02 to 2026-05-22 |
| Price | KRW 677,000 → KRW 1,941,000 |
| YTD return | <strong>+186.7%</strong> |
| Intraday high | KRW 1,995,000, May 15 |
| Foreign net buying | <strong>KRW -34.4T</strong> |
| Institutional net buying | KRW +9.2T |
| Retail net buying | KRW +24.1T |
| Foreign ownership | 53.81% → 51.66% |

SK hynix is the purer HBM beta, which explains why it outperformed Samsung. Foreign selling here does not mean the company is weak. It means a very successful position is being reduced.

The fundamentals remain powerful. SK hynix reported <strong>KRW 52.5763T</strong> in 1Q26 revenue, <strong>KRW 37.6103T</strong> in operating profit, and a <strong>72%</strong> operating margin, citing strong AI infrastructure demand and high-value memory products. ([SK hynix / PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/sk-hynix-announces-1q26-financial-results-302750959.html))

## Bottom Line

The right sentence is not “foreigners sold Korea.”

It is:

<strong>Foreigners sold Korea’s AI-memory mega-caps while selectively rotating into energy infrastructure, biotech, batteries, K-beauty, shipbuilding/defense and robotics.</strong>

That does not kill the Korea thesis. But it changes the trading discipline. Samsung Electronics and SK hynix are still core holdings, not fresh-cash priority buys until foreign buying returns or the stocks digest the distribution.
