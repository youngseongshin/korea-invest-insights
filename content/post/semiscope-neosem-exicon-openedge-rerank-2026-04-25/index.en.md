## TL;DR

1. <strong>Short-term momentum (the 2026 earnings inflection): Exicon > Neosem > OpenEdge.</strong> The consensus comp note that flags Exicon as an afterthought is reasoning purely through a CXL-purity prism. On a CLT-thesis prism — single-source DRAM low-frequency tester with Samsung's final qualification cleared in late 2024 — Exicon is the cleanest J-curve entry of the three.
2. <strong>The 2024-2025 loss / revenue-decline reset is the most under-reflected fact in the consensus comp.</strong> Neosem revenue fell 39.3% YoY in 2025 with operating margin compressed to 6.4%. Exicon ran a KRW 15.9B operating loss in 2024. OpenEdge's 3Q YTD operating loss of KRW 21.3B is *widening*, not shrinking. All three are in turnaround positioning — that means volatility before the inflection.
3. <strong>Long-term IP platform value (2027-2029): OpenEdge > Neosem > Exicon.</strong> Here the consensus is right. The caveat is that OpenEdge's royalty mix at 0.4% of revenue means the J-curve inflection sits further out than the framing implies.

<strong>Bottom line — by timeframe, not by ranking:</strong>

| Name | Position type | Entry timing | Key event |

## 2. Critical Re-Evaluation of the Consensus Comp

### 2-1. "OpenEdge has the deepest technology moat"

<strong>Partial agreement.</strong> The design-in switching cost on hard IP and the Memory Subsystem integration capability inside Korea is effectively monopolistic, and that part is fine. But against Synopsys and Cadence on a global comparison the gap is meaningful, and silicon-proven track record at leading nodes (N3/N2) is well behind.

<strong>"Deepest moat" should be read as "deepest moat within Korea."</strong> OpenEdge's moat reads less as a global multi-bagger candidate and more as a quality compounder anchored to the Korean AI/ASIC ecosystem.

### 2-2. "Neosem is the most genuinely irreplaceable name in CXL"

<strong>Agreed, conditionally.</strong> First-mover on CXL 1.1/2.0 plus the device-validation history with Samsung is the strongest single asset right now. But the comp under-weights the probability that <strong>CXL 3.1 introduces a dual-sourcing policy</strong>.

Implication: in a CXL 3.1 50:50 split scenario between Exicon and Neosem, Neosem's "irreplaceability premium" gets partially diluted.

### 2-3. "Exicon's Samsung relationship is strong but CXL purity and lead are weak — afterthought"

<strong>Partial agreement → active re-interpretation.</strong> The CXL-purity statement is factually correct, but this report views CLT — single-source, 11,520-parallel capacity, Samsung final-qual cleared in late 2024 — as forming a J-curve thesis <strong>independent of CXL</strong> as a Samsung DRAM back-end efficiency tool.

<strong>Through the CXL prism, Exicon is an afterthought. Through the DRAM back-end ATE prism, Exicon has the strongest near-term momentum.</strong> Both prisms are operating simultaneously, so Exicon should re-rank one tier higher than the consensus places it.

### 2-4. "Asymmetry: Neosem most balanced, OpenEdge long option, Exicon afterthought"

The framing collapses time horizons. Disaggregated:

- <strong>Short term (6-12 months) momentum: Exicon > Neosem > OpenEdge.</strong> Exicon's KRW 79B cumulative bookings + KRW 100B revenue guidance is the strongest signal.
- <strong>Mid term (1-2 years) CXL cycle: Neosem > Exicon > OpenEdge.</strong> Agree with the consensus.
- <strong>Long term (3-5 years) IP platform: OpenEdge > Neosem > Exicon.</strong> Agree with the consensus.

## 4. Scenario Analysis (All Three, Same Lens)

### Scenario A — Memory CapEx normalizes + CXL 3.1 ramps (Bull)

- Korean memory CapEx normalizes in 2026 → back-end ATE order cycle recovers.
- Samsung CXL 3.1 line goes live; dual-sourcing policy splits Neosem and Exicon.
- <strong>Beneficiary intensity: Exicon ≥ Neosem > OpenEdge</strong> — equipment names track cycle directly; OpenEdge is lagged by IP-to-volume conversion.

### Scenario B — AI inference ASIC + automotive ASIC explosion (Bull)

- Design-house diversification accelerates across AI inference and ADAS.
- LPDDR6 + UCIe adoption accelerates → OpenEdge license ASP compounds.
- <strong>Beneficiary intensity: OpenEdge > Neosem > Exicon</strong> — OpenEdge is the direct IP-diversification beneficiary; equipment names benefit only indirectly.

### Scenario C — Memory CapEx recovery delayed + R&D burn accelerates (Bear)

- Samsung CapEx slows, memory pricing stays soft.
- All three stay under R&D pressure with poor revenue visibility.
- <strong>Damage intensity: Neosem > Exicon ≥ OpenEdge</strong> — Neosem most exposed to cycle recovery; OpenEdge already in loss so marginal incremental damage is bounded.

### Scenario D — Global IP / ATE leaders accelerate Korea entry (Bear)

- Synopsys / Cadence expand share in Korean leading-node IP; Advantest re-enters SSD ATE.
- <strong>Damage intensity: OpenEdge > Neosem > Exicon</strong> — OpenEdge most exposed to global competition; Exicon's CLT 11,520-parallel barrier holds the longest.

## 6. Final Note (Allocator's Frame)

The consensus comp ranks the three names through a single prism — CXL substitutability — and through that prism the conclusion (Neosem = Core, OpenEdge = option, Exicon = afterthought) is internally coherent. <strong>But once you fold in the 2026 earnings data and the order-disclosure flow, the picture shifts.</strong>

- <strong>Exicon, viewed outside the CXL prism, is the strongest near-term momentum name in this trio.</strong> Single-source CLT, Samsung final-qual cleared in late 2024, KRW 79B cumulative bookings, and KRW 100B+ revenue guidance form a clean J-curve thesis. Low CXL exposure is not a weakness here — it is the strength of an <strong>independent thesis</strong> built on Samsung DRAM back-end efficiency.
- <strong>Neosem is a name where consensus has already reset once on the 2025 revenue collapse.</strong> 1Q26 is the first revenue-recovery confirmation print; before that, this remains a cycle bet. The first CXL 3.1 mass-production disclosure is the entry-confirmation signal.
- <strong>OpenEdge — agree with the consensus framing</strong>, but the 2025 widened loss and 0.4% royalty mix imply the J-curve inflection sits <strong>further out than the framing suggests</strong>. The 2026 BEP-or-not print is the watershed for the long-term valuation.

<strong>Final recommendation:</strong> Rather than collapsing the three names into a single 1-2-3 ranking, run them as <strong>time-horizon-separated bets</strong>. Exicon = short term, Neosem = mid term, OpenEdge = long-dated option. <strong>Layered exposure by timeframe</strong> delivers the best risk-adjusted setup of the available framings.

## Appendix — Exicon Deep Profile

### Snapshot

- Core products: CLT (Chambered Low-frequency memory Tester) at 11,520 parallel — next-gen DRAM stability tester; Gen5 SSD Tester (Samsung-supplied); Burn-in Tester; CXL 2.0 Tester; non-memory testers (SoC / CIS / DDI).
- Margins / track record: 2024 revenue KRW 31.6B / operating loss KRW -15.9B (loss-making). 1H 2025 cumulative operating loss KRW -8.6B. Cumulative new orders Oct 2025 - Mar 2026 ≈ KRW 79B (CLT / Burn-in / SSD). Both company and sell-side guide to first <strong>KRW 100B+ revenue year + full swing-to-profit in 2026</strong>.
- Technology moat: CLT 11,520-parallel processing (\~20x prior-gen 500-parallel). Samsung final qualification cleared in late 2024, sole supplier of LF tester since 2025; estimated technology lead at least 2 years vs competitors.
- Investment view: <strong>Event-driven turnaround cycle name.</strong> Exited the 2024 trough via single-source CLT and large-order J-curve entry. <strong>Highest exposure to Samsung DRAM CapEx among Korean back-end ATE names.</strong>

### Quantum-jump triggers

- <strong>Trigger #1 — CLT volume cycle entry + 20+ cumulative installs.</strong> Per company guidance, hitting \~20 cumulative CLT installs at Samsung DRAM lines in 2026 supports KRW 100B+ revenue. 2024 revenue KRW 31.6B → 2026 KRW 100B+ implies \~3.2x topline + swing-to-profit.
- <strong>Trigger #2 — CXL 3.1 dual-vendor entry.</strong> If Samsung adopts a dual-sourcing policy on CXL 3.1, Exicon could capture \~50% of equipment supply alongside Neosem. CXL ASP runs \~2-3x SSD Tester levels, implying meaningful incremental revenue.
- <strong>Trigger #3 — Gen6 SSD Tester R&D → volume.</strong> R&D-line Gen6 SSD Tester supply could materialize in 2026, with volume entry in 2027. Risk: Neosem retains pole position given Advantest exit.
- <strong>Trigger #4 — Non-memory (SoC / AP / DDI) tester volume recognition.</strong> CIS-tester-derived DDI / AP testers winning a Samsung S.LSI or external fabless design-in would meaningfully de-risk the cycle dependency.

### Risks & watchlist

- <strong>Single-customer concentration.</strong> Revenue is overwhelmingly Samsung-dependent — 2024's -61.6% revenue decline is the cleanest illustration. Any Samsung CapEx slowdown flows directly into a single quarter.
- <strong>CLT competitor catch-up.</strong> No competitor is currently visible at 11,520-parallel chamber engineering, but a 2-year window is not infinite; monitor Dlight / UnTest Sci (and others) for entry signals.
- <strong>CXL 3.1 first-mover not secured.</strong> Neosem retains the lead in CXL. If Exicon stays second-source through CXL 3.1, the CXL-cycle alpha is capped.

### Five checkpoints for the next 1-2 quarters

1. <strong>1Q26 print — magnitude of the swing to profit.</strong> How much of the KRW 79B order book recognizes as revenue and operating profit. The cleanest stability check.
2. <strong>Cumulative CLT install count for 2026.</strong> Company guides to \~20 units. Quarterly tracking of whether installs follow the trajectory.
3. <strong>CXL 3.1 Tester Samsung qualification disclosure timing.</strong> Most likely 1H 2026. Pass = dual-source seat secured. Miss = no CXL-cycle participation.
4. <strong>HBM-line entry for inspection equipment.</strong> Whether HBM4E back-end-validation burden flows into CLT / Burn-in demand. A formal HBM-spec disclosure from the company would be the meaningful signal.
5. <strong>Non-memory (DDI / AP) tester revenue disclosure.</strong> A separate disclosure with the line item exceeding 10% of revenue is the quantitative diversification signal.
