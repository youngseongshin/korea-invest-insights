## First, remember just two words

Two words keep showing up in investing, and they are the whole point of this post.

* **Catalyst = the trigger that can lift a stock.** A drug approval, a licensing deal, a profit surge — the kind of event where "if this lands, the stock moves."
* **Undervalued = cheap.** It means that trigger is **still not fully priced in**.

Here is the key. **"The catalyst is alive" and "it's cheap right now" are different statements.** Even a great company is a tough new buy if the good news is already stuffed into the price. Conversely, an ordinary-looking name can be an opportunity if the good news is still under-priced.

1. **For all six names the catalyst (trigger) is alive.** None of them is dead.

3. **Samsung Biologics and Alteogen** are great companies but already in expensive territory; **Hanmi** still needs launch proof; **Oscotec** is a "lottery-ticket" name that pays big if it works.

## The six, in plain terms

### 1. SK Biopharm — "a company that already sells a drug and makes money"

The easiest name to grasp. While other biotechs sell the dream of "someday, if a drug arrives…," SK Biopharm **already sells the epilepsy drug 'Xcopri (cenobamate)' in the US and earns from it.**

Q1 revenue was **about KRW 227.9bn** (+57.8% YoY) and operating profit **about KRW 89.8bn** (+249.7%), a record quarter. Xcopri US sales alone were **about KRW 197.7bn** (+48.4%). Because the company built its own US sales force, profits now grow faster than sales as volume rises (operating leverage).

**Cheap?** On the morning of June 11 the stock was **about KRW 84,000**, just above its 1-year low (\~KRW 81,000) and far from its 1-year high (\~KRW 144,000). Profits are climbing fast yet the price sits near the bottom, so **it is not expensive relative to its earnings growth.** The market still discounts it as "a risky biotech reliant on a single product" — and that looks like the opportunity.

**Risks:** single-product reliance on Xcopri, slowing US prescription growth, cost of bringing in a second product.

**Call:** very strong catalyst + undervalued → **#1 for fresh money (accumulate in tranches).**

### 2. ST Pharm — "the RNA-drug factory with orders piling up"

ST Pharm does not bet on whether a trial succeeds. It is a **contract maker (CDMO) of RNA/oligonucleotides (the core raw material of gene-based drugs).** Whether the drug succeeds or not, **it earns when orders to make that drug come in.** It is closer to an industrial (a factory) than to a biotech.

The key now is that **orders are piling up.** Q1 operating profit was **about KRW 11.5bn**, up a massive **+1,024%** YoY, and the oligo order backlog (orders already booked) is **about KRW 340bn–460bn** (varies by source), of which **over 80% is actual commercial volume, not clinical.** In January it signed an oligo supply deal with a US pharma and in March one with a European pharma, each worth **KRW 80bn–90bn**. It also completed a second plant (Oligo Building 2) last September.

**Cheap?** On the June 11 morning the stock was **about KRW 117,000** (1-year high \~KRW 172,000). On a simple trailing PER (\~43x) it looks pricey, but that is an **illusion.** PER uses "past 12 months' earnings"; as the backlog converts to revenue and profit through 2026–2027 and plant utilization rises, earnings grow quickly. Analysts see this year's operating profit up **+42%** and call the share-price drop overdone.

**Risks:** undisclosed clients / order concentration, early plant fixed costs, quarter-to-quarter shipment timing.

**Call:** strong catalyst + conditional undervaluation → **#2 for fresh money (accumulate in tranches).**

### 3. Hanmi — "an obesity-drug bet; trust restored, but wait for launch proof"

Hanmi's trigger is **obesity / metabolic drugs.** It recently **out-licensed a candidate (sonefpeglutide) to global pharma Eli Lilly** — total deal about **USD 1.26bn**, with a non-refundable upfront of **USD 75m**. A big company buying Hanmi's technology signals **restored trust** in its R&D.

The next gate is Hanmi's own obesity drugs. Its in-house obesity candidate (efpeglenatide) has filed for Korean approval, targeting a **2H 2026 domestic launch**, while a next-generation candidate (HM15275, etc.) is in Phase 2.

**Cheap?** On the June 11 morning the stock was **around KRW 390,000s** (1-year high \~KRW 647,000). The pullback was large, but because **the stock already rose once right after the June Lilly deal**, calling this "the bottom" is early. The bigger issue: the obesity market is already crowded with strong players like Wegovy and Zepbound. A "homegrown GLP-1" story alone is not enough — **actual approval, pricing and early prescription numbers** must show up.

**Call:** strong catalyst + right after the event → **wait for launch / data (Wait).**

### 4. Oscotec — "royalties hold the floor; the new out-license is a lottery"

Think of Oscotec as having **two engines.** One is **already paying** — the lung-cancer drug 'Leclaza (lazertinib)' sells globally and brings in **royalties**, which support the floor under the stock. The other is a **lottery engine** — it out-licensed its candidate (cevidoplenib) to US-based Agios. Total deal up to **USD 600m+**, upfront **USD 25m** (Oscotec's share about 75%).

One honest caveat: cevidoplenib **failed to hit its primary endpoint statistically in Phase 2.** Some secondary readouts were good and the partner is preparing the next step (Phase 3), but the **Phase 3 entry target is 2028**, so it is far out and failure risk remains.

**Cheap?** June 11 morning price **about KRW 38,500** (1-year high \~KRW 66,000), market cap \~KRW 1.47tn. On earnings (PER) it is not a cheap stock. It is an **option-type name where you buy the stable Leclaza royalty plus the cevidoplenib/new-pipeline lottery.**

**Call:** catalyst alive but high risk → **Watchlist; small size / diversify as a rule.**

### 5. Samsung Biologics — "the #1 contract manufacturer; great but already expensive"

Samsung Biologics' trigger is not a trial but **factories.** It is the **#1 contract maker (CDMO)** of biologics for the world's drugmakers. Q1 revenue was **about KRW 1.2571tn** and operating profit **about KRW 580.8bn**, with Plants 1–4 at full run, Plant 5 starting up, the US (Rockville) plant acquisition (total capacity expanded to 845,000 liters), and a dedicated ADC (antibody-drug conjugate, a next-gen drug) facility — the growth fuel is clearly alive.

**Cheap?** This is the point. June 11 morning price **about KRW 1.26m**, market cap **about KRW 58tn**, PER \~28x. It is off its 1-year high (\~KRW 1.98m), but this company is already valued as **"a world-class, premium contract manufacturer."** So it is not "a cheap biotech" but **"a good company that pulled back a little."** There is upside, but it is unlikely to re-rate as fast as a drug-approval winner.

**Call:** catalyst alive but not undervalued (premium) → **buy on dips (Wait).**

### 6. Alteogen — "the strongest catalyst, but expectations are already loaded"

Alteogen owns a **platform (ALT-B4) that converts intravenous (IV) cancer drugs into subcutaneous (SC) injections.** A shot that took hours in hospital becomes quick, so drugmakers line up. In fact the SC version of Merck (MSD)'s mega cancer drug **'Keytruda' won US FDA approval**, with deals following from AstraZeneca, GSK, Biogen, Daiichi Sankyo, Sandoz and others. By catalyst strength, it is the **strongest of the six.**

**Cheap?** No. June 11 morning price **about KRW 303,000**, market cap **about KRW 16tn**, PER a striking **\~120x**. The expectation that "huge royalties are coming" is already loaded into the price. On top of that, a recent Merck disclosure showed the Keytruda SC **royalty rate at around 2%**, below the 4–5% the market hoped, trimming expectations. Patent litigation (the dispute with Halozyme) is another variable.

In short, Alteogen is **not "cheap"; it is a stock that gets re-priced every time Keytruda SC sales and royalties show up in the numbers.**

**Call:** strongest catalyst but already expensive → **cautious new buy (wait for a price reset / royalty confirmation).**

## So what's the priority?

Even with the same "catalyst alive," once you also weigh **"is it cheap?"** the order shapes up like this.

1. **Worth accumulating now (catalyst + undervalued):** SK Biopharm, ST Pharm
2. **R&D-hope type (after confirmation):** Hanmi
3. **Lottery type (high risk / high reward):** Oscotec
4. **Large, stable type (on dips):** Samsung Biologics
5. **Platform-hope type (already expensive):** Alteogen

In one sentence: **"A live catalyst and a cheap price are different things. The two that satisfy both are SK Biopharm and ST Pharm."**

## Checkpoints that would change the call

If the numbers below change, the conclusions above change too. When the news drops, watch only these.

* **SK Biopharm:** Xcopri US sales growth (holding 30%+ per quarter?), operating margin (defending 30%+?).
* **ST Pharm:** Q2–Q3 oligo shipment normalization, backlog held/growing, rising plant utilization.
* **Hanmi:** obesity-drug approval / pricing / early prescriptions; next-gen candidate (HM15275, etc.) data.
* **Oscotec:** are Leclaza royalties growing each quarter; is the cevidoplenib Phase 3 schedule confirmed.
* **Samsung Biologics:** Plant 5 revenue ramp, US (Rockville) contribution, pace of new large orders.
* **Alteogen:** actual Keytruda SC conversion rate / quarterly royalty amount, direction of the patent suit.

## The bottom line

Among these six, **no catalyst (trigger) is dead.** The issue, as always, is **price.** You don't buy a good company at any price — you pick **the name where the good news is still under-priced.** That principle from our last bio post applies again here. On today's read, the two that best meet that test are **SK Biopharm and ST Pharm.**

## FAQ

**Q. The catalyst is "alive," so why not buy?**
A. Even with a live trigger, if the good news is already fully in the price, there is little left for a new buyer. That is Alteogen and Samsung Biologics. A good company is not the same as a cheap stock.

**Q. So will SK Biopharm and ST Pharm definitely rise?**
A. No. It means they are "more attractive on catalyst-vs-price than the other four," not a guarantee. SK Biopharm carries single-product (Xcopri) reliance and ST Pharm has quarterly shipment swings. That is why the rule is to buy **in tranches**, not all at once.

**Q. ST Pharm is at PER 43x — how is that cheap?**
A. PER uses "last 12 months' earnings," which creates an illusion. ST Pharm is at the start of converting its backlog into revenue and profit, so **on forward earnings** it may not be as expensive as today's PER.

**Q. Why is Oscotec a "lottery"?**
A. The Leclaza royalty is stable, but the out-licensed cevidoplenib missed its Phase 2 primary endpoint and its next step is in 2028. The payoff is big if it works, but failure risk is high — hence small size and diversification.

**Q. Is this a buy recommendation?**
A. No. It is not individual buy/sell advice but a research lens that **separates "catalyst alive" from "undervalued."** Invest on your own judgment and responsibility.
