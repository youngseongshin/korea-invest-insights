## 2. Why the Question Keeps Coming Up

Olive Young grew from a domestic chain into a <strong>global K-beauty distribution platform</strong> in the post-COVID window:

- <strong>Domestic dominance</strong> after rivals (Lalavla / GS, Lohbs / Lotte) effectively exited Korea's H&B chain category — Olive Young is now the de-facto national H&B utility.
- <strong>Inbound-tourist halo</strong> — the Myeongdong flagship became a mandatory stop for Chinese, Japanese, and Southeast Asian travelers; tax-refund volume surged.
- <strong>Cross-border digital channel</strong> — `globalolive.young.co.kr` ships to 150+ countries, and the brand actively cross-promotes on TikTok / Instagram in the US, Japan, Vietnam, and Indonesia.
- <strong>PB / exclusive-brand engine</strong> — Olive Young is no longer a pure retailer; it is also a <strong>brand incubator</strong> (e.g., wakemake, Bring Green, Round A'Round) and an <strong>exclusive-launch channel</strong> for emerging K-beauty brands wanting domestic shelf-test before global scale-up.

This is exactly the profile US / EU / SEA retail investors want to own. The friction: <strong>there is no Olive Young ticker.</strong>

## 3. The Cleanest Indirect Proxy: CJ Corporation (001040.KS)

CJ Corporation is the listed holding company at the top of the CJ Group. Its ownership in CJ Olive Young is the largest single block.

<strong>Approximate cap-table (publicly reported levels)</strong>

| Holder | Stake (approx.) |
|---|---:|
| CJ Corporation (001040.KS) | <strong>\~51.2%</strong> |
| Lee Sun-ho (chairman's son) | \~11.0% |
| Lee Kyung-hoo (chairman's daughter) | \~4.6% |
| Glenwood / Korean financial investors (2021 pre-IPO round) | balance, with secondary trades since |

(Stake percentages are reported levels and have shifted with each capital event; the directional fact — CJ Corp majority + Lee-family meaningful, financial-investor float — is stable.)

<strong>What CJ Corp actually owns (beyond Olive Young)</strong>

CJ Corp is a holdco. Its NAV is the sum of stakes in:

- <strong>CJ CheilJedang</strong> (food / bio — Bibigo, fermented amino acids)
- <strong>CJ ENM</strong> (entertainment — TVING, Mnet, Studio Dragon)
- <strong>CJ Logistics</strong>
- <strong>CJ Olive Young</strong> (Korea's #1 H&B)
- <strong>CJ Freshway</strong> (B2B food)
- Smaller affiliates

So owning CJ Corp gives you <strong>a holdco-discounted slice of Olive Young plus everything else.</strong> That is both the appeal and the limitation.

<strong>Holdco discount math (illustrative)</strong>

If you wanted *only* Olive Young exposure, CJ Corp gives you:

```text
Effective Olive Young exposure per ₩ of CJ Corp
= CJ Corp ownership in OY (~51.2%)
  × your ownership in CJ Corp
  × (1 − holdco discount)
```

Korean holdcos historically trade at a <strong>30–50% discount</strong> to NAV. So even if Olive Young's standalone equity value rises, your CJ Corp share captures only a discounted portion. Conversely, when the market re-rates Korean holdcos (e.g., during a Value-Up or governance-reform cycle — see April 2026 "Korea Outperformance" backdrop), CJ Corp can outperform Olive Young's underlying growth via discount compression.

## 5. Olive Young IPO History — Why It Hasn't Happened Yet

A short timeline:

- <strong>2019</strong> — CJ Olive Young separates from CJ Olive Networks via spin-off, becomes its own corporate entity.
- <strong>2021</strong> — Pre-IPO round closes; financial investors (Glenwood-led consortium reported) take a minority stake; valuation discussions reportedly in the <strong>₩1.5–1.8T</strong> range at the time.
- <strong>2022</strong> — Olive Young files for KOSPI listing. The deal is <strong>withdrawn</strong> citing weak IPO market conditions and valuation gap.
- <strong>2023–2024</strong> — Reports surface periodically of strategic options including a <strong>block-sale of the Lee-family stake</strong>, an Olive Young secondary offering, or a <strong>renewed IPO timing window</strong>. Some reports mentioned <strong>Goldman Sachs</strong> as advisor on capital-structure options. None of these culminated in a confirmed listing.
- <strong>2026 (current)</strong> — Olive Young remains private. Operating numbers continue to grow strongly (revenue past ₩4T-class, double-digit OPM range). <strong>No confirmed listing date.</strong>

<strong>Why the delay matters for investors</strong>

1. <strong>Valuation has likely moved up.</strong> The 2021 pre-IPO mark of ₩1.5–1.8T is dated. Every additional year of double-digit revenue + margin expansion lifts the implied IPO valuation, which expands CJ Corp's NAV — but only realizes if the market re-rates the holdco.
2. <strong>Successor-generation ownership matters.</strong> Lee Sun-ho's \~11% personal stake is non-trivial. Any future IPO + post-IPO secondary creates a path to <strong>family-wealth crystallization</strong> that is a structural incentive for CJ Group to push the listing eventually.
3. <strong>Korean Value-Up backdrop.</strong> With Korean governance reform and the broader 2026 re-rating story (KOSPI YTD strength, holdco discount compression), the <strong>window for Olive Young IPO has materially improved</strong> vs. the 2022 environment.

<strong>Implication:</strong> the right way to read CJ Corp today is <strong>"holdco that owns a yet-to-IPO crown-jewel asset that is roughly 50% bigger and structurally better-positioned than at the last failed IPO attempt."</strong>

## 8. Risks to the "Buy CJ Corp for Olive Young" Trade

This trade is not a free option. Specific risks:

1. <strong>Holdco discount stays wide.</strong> If Korean Value-Up momentum fades, holdco discounts can stay at 35–50% indefinitely, capping how much Olive Young value flows through to CJ Corp shareholders.
2. <strong>No IPO crystallization.</strong> Without a listing, Olive Young's "marked" value remains an internal accounting figure. Public-market re-rating only happens with a public catalyst (IPO, large secondary, strategic spin-off).
3. <strong>CJ Corp's other assets dilute.</strong> CJ ENM and CJ CheilJedang carry their own cyclical pressures (entertainment cycle, lysine pricing, fx). A bad year at the affiliate level can offset Olive Young's accretion.
4. <strong>Family-stake overhang.</strong> Lee-family direct stakes in Olive Young (separate from CJ Corp's stake) mean a future IPO has potential <strong>family-block secondary supply</strong> that markets may price in.
5. <strong>Inbound-tourism beta.</strong> Olive Young is structurally exposed to Chinese-visitor recovery; downside scenarios on Korea–China relations affect a real % of the asset's growth.
6. <strong>Channel disruption.</strong> US TikTok / Amazon dynamics could either accelerate (if Olive Young's globalolive channel scales) or threaten (if independent Korean brands route around Olive Young directly to Western retailers).

## FAQ — Olive Young

<strong>Q: Is Olive Young publicly traded?</strong>
A: No. Olive Young (CJ Olive Young Corp.) is not listed on any stock exchange. The cleanest indirect proxy is <strong>CJ Corporation (KOSPI: 001040)</strong>, which owns approximately 51.2% of CJ Olive Young.

<strong>Q: Who owns Olive Young?</strong>
A: CJ Corporation (001040.KS) is the controlling shareholder (~51.2%). Lee Sun-ho (chairman Lee Jay-hyun's son, ~11%) and Lee Kyung-hoo (daughter, ~5%) hold direct minority stakes. Financial investors hold the remainder via the 2021 pre-IPO round.

<strong>Q: When did Olive Young's IPO get postponed?</strong>

<strong>Q: How much revenue does Olive Young generate?</strong>

<strong>Q: Is Olive Young coming to the US?</strong>

<strong>Q: Is CJ CheilJedang the same as Olive Young?</strong>

<strong>Q: What is the relationship between CJ Group and Olive Young?</strong>
A: Olive Young (CJ Olive Young Corp.) is a CJ Group affiliate. The listed CJ Group holding company, <strong>CJ Corporation (001040.KS)</strong>, is the majority shareholder of CJ Olive Young.
