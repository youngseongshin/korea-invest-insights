## TL;DR

- This issue should be framed around two questions: **(1) did Samsung internally acknowledge a memory supercycle through 2028? (2) how much additional treasury-stock buying could the special-bonus structure create?**
- The answer to the first question is **conditional yes**. If the hurdle is annual DS operating profit of KRW 200 trillion, it is a strong internal signal on HBM4, server DRAM and eSSD durability through 2028. If it is cumulative 2026-2028 profit of KRW 200 trillion, the signal is much weaker.
- The answer to the second question depends heavily on the denominator. The reported KRW 80 trillion is not an official fixed amount. It looks like an estimate based on KRW 1,220 trillion of 2026-2028 consolidated operating-profit forecasts, a 10.5% pool and a 60% after-tax payout assumption.
- If the headline net KRW 80 trillion is correct, it would buy roughly **252 million shares** at KRW 317,500, or about **4.3%** of common shares outstanding. But this is **employee compensation stock**, not a cancellation-style shareholder-return program.
- The event is best read as **internal supercycle signaling + HBM talent retention + near-term float absorption + future overhang**, not simply as a larger shareholder-return announcement.

## 1. The Two Questions

The market headline has focused on the phrase "Samsung may buy KRW 80 trillion of shares for special bonuses." That is too blunt.

The investment question should be reduced to two parts:

1. **Does this mean Samsung internally recognizes a DS and memory supercycle through 2028?**
2. **How much additional treasury-stock buying could be created by the special-bonus structure?**

The first question is about **earnings-cycle durability**: how long Samsung sees the HBM4, server DRAM, enterprise SSD and SOCAMM2 demand cycle lasting.

The second question is about **flow and capital-policy mechanics**: how much stock the company might need to buy, and whether those shares are cancelled or paid to employees.

## 3. Question 1: Did Samsung Acknowledge a Supercycle Through 2028?

The answer is **conditional yes**.

Samsung has not officially declared "a supercycle through 2028." But if the reported bonus hurdle is tied to annual DS operating profit of KRW 200 trillion, it becomes a strong internal signal.

That is because 1Q26 DS operating profit was KRW 53.7 trillion.

```text
1Q26 DS OP KRW 53.7tn x 4 = annualized KRW 214.8tn
```

Annual KRW 200 trillion is not fantasy. It is close to the current run-rate.

### 3.1 If the Hurdle Is Annual KRW 200tn, It Matters

If the agreement says DS must earn around KRW 200 trillion each year from 2026 to 2028, the implication is clear.

Samsung may be internally assuming that:

1. HBM4 and HBM4E qualification and ramp stay broadly on track.
2. Server DRAM and enterprise SSD demand persists beyond a one-year spike.
3. NVIDIA Vera Rubin, next-generation GPUs/CPUs and hyperscaler AI capex keep improving the memory mix.
4. AI and server-memory mix improvement offsets ordinary DRAM supply growth.

In that case, the special-bonus plan is not just a labor settlement.

It is a sign that **Samsung designed DS incentives around a high-profit memory environment lasting through 2028**.

### 3.2 If the Hurdle Is Cumulative KRW 200tn, the Signal Is Weak

If the condition is cumulative 2026-2028 DS operating profit of KRW 200 trillion, the interpretation changes completely.

```text
Cumulative KRW 200tn / 3 years = annual average KRW 66.7tn
```

Compared with the annualized 1Q26 DS run-rate of KRW 214.8 trillion, that is a low hurdle. Under this interpretation, the plan is closer to a compensation formula than a strong supercycle signal.

So the key is **annual versus cumulative**.

| Interpretation | DS Profit Hurdle | Versus 1Q26 Run-Rate | Investment Meaning |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Annual KRW 200tn | KRW 200tn each year | Close to annualized KRW 214.8tn | Strong internal signal on a 2026-2028 supercycle |
| Cumulative 2026-2028 KRW 200tn | Annual average KRW 66.7tn | Far below the current run-rate | Weak as a supercycle signal |
| Annual KRW 100tn for 2029-2035 | Long-term high-profit condition | Above many past cycle levels | Structural profitability claim after the peak cycle |

### 3.3 Official Commentary Already Confirms 2026 Strength

Samsung's official 1Q26 release is enough to confirm that management sees strong 2026 AI memory demand.

The company reported DS operating profit of KRW 53.7 trillion and explained that memory results were driven by AI demand, limited supply, industry-wide price increases and mass-product sales of HBM4 and SOCAMM2. It also said it plans to provide HBM4E samples in Q2 and expects server-memory demand to remain strong in the second half due to hyperscaler and enterprise AI/LLM adoption.

But that official release is not a 2028 guidance statement. The 2028 signal can only be inferred from the special-bonus formula.

The clean conclusion:

> Samsung did not officially declare a supercycle through 2028. But if the bonus hurdle is annual DS operating profit of KRW 200 trillion, the plan can be read as a strong internal signal that Samsung sees the high-profit memory cycle lasting through 2026-2028.

## 4. Question 2: How Much More Stock Could Samsung Buy?

The second question is numerical.

The reported KRW 80 trillion figure appears to come from this bridge:

```text
Estimated net treasury-stock purchase
= 2026-2028 operating-profit forecast x 10.5% x after-tax payout ratio
```

### 4.1 Headline Case: Consolidated Operating Profit

Using the operating-profit forecasts cited in Korean coverage:

| Item | 2026E | 2027E | 2028E | Total |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Consolidated OP forecast | KRW 352tn | KRW 444tn | KRW 424tn | KRW 1,220tn |
| 10.5% pool | KRW 37.0tn | KRW 46.6tn | KRW 44.5tn | KRW 128.1tn |
| 60% after-tax assumption | KRW 22.2tn | KRW 28.0tn | KRW 26.7tn | KRW 76.9tn |

So the "KRW 130tn gross / KRW 80tn net" headline is broadly consistent with the math.

But this only holds if the denominator is consolidated operating profit. If the final agreement uses DS operating profit, adjusted operating profit or pre-bonus operating profit, the number changes.

### 4.2 What Does Net KRW 80tn Mean in Shares?

Dividing headline net KRW 80 trillion by the cited May 27 price of KRW 317,500:

```text
KRW 80tn / KRW 317,500 = roughly 252mn shares
```

Against roughly 5.846bn common shares, that is about 4.3%.

| Assumption | Net Purchase Amount | Shares at KRW 317,500 | % of Common Shares |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Headline net | KRW 80.0tn | \~252mn | \~4.3% |
| Consolidated OP x 10.5% x 60% | KRW 76.9tn | \~242mn | \~4.1% |
| DS annual KRW 200tn x 3 years x 10.5% x 60% | KRW 37.8tn | \~119mn | \~2.0% |
| Cumulative DS KRW 200tn x 10.5% x 60% | KRW 12.6tn | \~40mn | \~0.7% |

This is the important table.

**KRW 80 trillion is closer to an upper-end scenario. Depending on the denominator, the actual incremental stock purchase could be closer to the low-teens trillion won.**

### 4.3 Compared With Existing Buybacks

Samsung's official IR page shows roughly KRW 10.0 trillion of buybacks over 2024-2025: KRW 1.8118 trillion in 2024 and KRW 8.1893 trillion in 2025. ([Samsung IR](https://www.samsung.com/global/ir/stock-information/shareholder-return/))

If special-bonus stock purchases reach the headline net KRW 76.9-80.0 trillion, they would be about 7.7-8.0x the official 2024-2025 buyback amount.

| Comparison | Amount |
|---|---:|
| Official 2024-2025 buybacks | \~KRW 10.0tn |
| Headline net special-bonus purchase estimate | \~KRW 76.9-80.0tn |
| Multiple | \~7.7-8.0x |

That is meaningful for flow.

But the critical point is that the shares are not being cancelled. They are being paid to employees.

## 5. Shareholder Return or Employee Compensation?

The precise answer is employee compensation.

During the purchase phase, the company buying shares can absorb float and support near-term flow. But those shares are later transferred to employees, not retired.

The economics have three stages:

| Stage | Effect | Shareholder Read |
|---|---|---|
| Company buys shares in the market | Float absorption and buying flow | Near-term positive |
| Shares are paid to employees | Treasury stock becomes individually held stock | No cancellation effect |
| Lockup expires or tradable portion is sold | Some shares can return to market | Future overhang |

A cancellation-style buyback permanently reduces shares outstanding and increases EPS. Employee-compensation stock does not. It can eventually return to the market, and voting rights revive once treasury shares are transferred to individuals.

That does not make the event bad. It simply means the objective is different:

1. Retain HBM-critical employees.
2. Link DS performance to employee compensation.
3. Give employees exposure to Samsung's share price.
4. Reduce immediate cash-bonus pressure.
5. Reduce labor risk.

This is not primarily a shareholder-return event. It is an **HBM execution and talent-retention event**.

## 6. Why Stock Compensation Matters for HBM

HBM competitiveness is not only about capex.

Samsung's HBM4 and HBM4E recovery depends on:

* customer qualification
* TSV process stability
* packaging yield
* base-die and foundry integration
* thermal and power characteristics
* NVIDIA and custom-ASIC qualification
* mix shift across enterprise SSD, SOCAMM2 and DDR5

The bottleneck is people as much as equipment. Stock compensation can be read as a long-duration incentive designed to retain DS talent through 2028.

This links the two questions:

```text
Samsung expects high DS profits through 2028
  ↓
It allocates part of that profit to employees in stock
  ↓
Employees are exposed to Samsung's share price and DS execution
  ↓
Samsung gets HBM talent retention and lower labor risk
```

For investors, the structure is two-sided.

| Opportunity | Risk |
|---|---|
| HBM execution recovery, talent retention, reduced labor risk | Higher compensation cost, labor receives part of cycle upside, future overhang |
| Near-term purchase flow | Not EPS-accretive like cancellation |
| Internal supercycle signal | Signal weakens if the KRW 200tn hurdle is cumulative rather than annual |

## 7. Final Answer to the Two Questions

### Question 1. Did Samsung Internally Recognize a Supercycle Through 2028?

**Not as an official declaration. But it can be a strong internal signal under the annual-profit interpretation.**

* If the hurdle is annual DS operating profit of KRW 200tn: **close to yes.** 1Q26 DS annualized run-rate was KRW 214.8tn, and embedding that kind of target in compensation through 2028 signals confidence in a high-profit memory cycle.
* If the hurdle is cumulative 2026-2028 DS operating profit of KRW 200tn: **closer to no.** Annual average KRW 66.7tn is far below the current run-rate, making it more of a compensation threshold than a supercycle signal.

The final agreement text is the key.

### Question 2. How Much Additional Stock Could Samsung Buy?

**Headline case: KRW 76.9-80.0tn, roughly 242-252mn shares, or about 4.1-4.3% of common shares.**

But the range is wide:

| Scenario | Estimated Stock Purchase | % of Common Shares |
|---|---:|---:|
| Cumulative DS KRW 200tn basis | \~KRW 12.6tn | \~0.7% |
| DS annual KRW 200tn x 3 years | \~KRW 37.8tn | \~2.0% |
| Consolidated OP forecast KRW 1,220tn | \~KRW 76.9tn | \~4.1% |
| Headline net KRW 80tn | \~KRW 80.0tn | \~4.3% |

The most important sentence:

> These are not cancellation shares. They are employee-compensation shares. The purchase phase can be positive for flow, but the shares can become future overhang after they are paid to employees.

So the core of the event is not "more shareholder return."

It is **how long Samsung sees the DS/HBM supercycle lasting, and how much of that upside it is willing to use to retain key HBM talent in stock**.
