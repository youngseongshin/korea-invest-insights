- Hanwha Ocean should no longer be viewed as a simple shipbuilder. It is a structure that <strong>earns through LNG carriers, receives multiples from special vessels/defense, and commands long-term option value from overseas MRO/naval defense platforms</strong>.

- Therefore, the current action call is <strong>fundamentally positive, technically needs confirmation</strong>. This is closer to riding a structural bull axis than buying undervaluation, and pullback/event confirmation is preferable to chasing.

## 3. The Core Investment Narrative — Three Layers

### Layer A: LNG Carrier Cycle

- US/Qatar LNG terminal capacity expansion
- Aging LNG fleet replacement demand
- Eco-regulation compliance

This is the traditional shipbuilding narrative. <strong>It underpins the earnings floor.</strong>

### Layer B: Special Vessels / Naval Defense

- Canadian submarine program
- KDDX and domestic/overseas naval projects
- High-value-add special vessel business

This is the axis that <strong>creates multiple re-rating</strong>, not just revenue. This is why the market has started viewing Hanwha Ocean differently from HD Hyundai's conventional shipbuilding.

### Layer C: Overseas MRO / Philly Shipyard / Global Naval Maritime Platform

Keywords confirmed from IR materials:
- <strong>Global Ocean Defense Company</strong>
- Overseas production bases
- Overseas MRO
- Unmanned/advanced naval technology

Hanwha Ocean is not simply "a company that builds ships well" — it is clearly <strong>positioning itself as the maritime axis of Hanwha Group's defense chain</strong>.

## 8. Peer Comparison: Hanwha Ocean vs HD Korea Shipbuilding vs Samsung Heavy Industries

### HD Korea Shipbuilding

- <strong>Narrative:</strong> The most orthodox blue-chip of the shipbuilding cycle. Diversified across LNG, container, tanker vessel types.
- <strong>Strength:</strong> Most balanced business portfolio. Cleanest pure-play bet on "shipbuilding itself."
- <strong>Weakness:</strong> Lacks the defense/special vessel/maritime security premium relative to Hanwha Ocean. Closer to a cycle normalization play than a multiple re-rating story.
- <strong>Verdict:</strong> The textbook good company. But less aggressive narrative than Hanwha Ocean for justifying further premium.

### Samsung Heavy Industries

- <strong>Narrative:</strong> LNG/FLNG/offshore projects. Earnings leverage from project wins.
- <strong>Strength:</strong> Strong leverage when specific projects materialize. Clear LNG/FLNG exposure.
- <strong>Weakness:</strong> Still carries project-driven/individual order volatility. Lower qualitative stability and semi-core concentration tolerance.
- <strong>Verdict:</strong> Good for trading and cyclical leverage, but one tier below Hanwha Ocean/HD Korea Shipbuilding for large semi-core positions.

### Hanwha Ocean

- <strong>Narrative:</strong> Earns through LNG carriers, receives multiples from special vessels/defense, commands long-term optionality from overseas MRO/naval bases/maritime defense platforms.
- <strong>Premium Source:</strong> Special vessels/submarines, naval defense exports, overseas MRO, integration with Hanwha's defense chain.
- <strong>Premium Validity:</strong> Partially justified. But maintaining/expanding requires Canada, KDDX, overseas MRO, and special vessel orders to actually materialize.
