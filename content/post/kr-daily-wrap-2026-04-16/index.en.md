## Market Wrap

Today's Korea stock market session (April 10 close, most recent briefing) was best described as **compression, not expansion** — a risk-on tape with pockets of overheating rather than a broad-based advance.

The macro backdrop was permissive: USD/KRW five-day softening, declining Brent, and a VIX below 20 all pointed toward a reduced risk premium. Yet the session was not one where "anything goes." Strong capital flows concentrated in three narrow themes while the majority of speculative setups failed to hold intraday levels.

**Strong themes:**
- **Semiconductor & AI hardware** led the session. Samsung Electro-Mechanics (삼성전기, 009150.KS) and Samsung Electronics (삼성전자, 005930.KS) were the core anchors, with the rally reading as a value-chain rotation into AI hardware rather than a simple bounce.
- **Telecom infrastructure** was the day's most visible momentum cluster. Daehan Optical Cable (대한광통신, 010060.KS), Solid (쏠리드, 050890.KS), RFHIC (알에프에이치아이씨, 218410.KS), and KMW (케이엠더블유, 032500.KS) posted sharp gains with consistent telegram-channel chatter and broker flow. The RF/5G infrastructure buildout narrative appears to be broadening.
- **Construction & Middle East reconstruction** received fresh broker upgrades. Hyundai Engineering & Construction (현대건설, 000720.KS) was raised to Buy with a ₩240,000 target at Hana Securities on Q2 sentiment improvement and reconstruction pipeline. NH maintained a Positive sector view. However, intraday execution data showed most construction entries — including Daewoo E&C (대우건설, 047040.KS) — failed to hold VWAP or reference price by close, limiting real follow-through.
- **Power & energy** saw SMP (System Marginal Price) surge +47% in headlines, with ESS contract speculation circulating. Structural interest remains valid, but direct portfolio-linked names were limited.

**Weak / underperforming areas:**
- Biotech underperformed broadly. Multiple internal notes flagged a "reduce biotech exposure, compress into IT/semiconductor component names" direction — sector rotation at the margin away from bio.
- Mid-session breakout setups in hot themes (construction, some telecom) saw meaningful failure rates, with VWAP breaks confirming the selective character.

**Flow signals:** Foreign buying in Samsung Electronics continued to be the most cited institutional flow anchor. In telecom infrastructure, the move appeared retail-momentum-driven rather than confirmed institutional accumulation — a distinction that matters for durability.

Bottom line: today's market rewarded existing leaders and punished late chasers. The regime remains Neutral; the session character was "good names get better" rather than "rising tide lifts all boats."

### Top 3 Deep Dive

**#1 — SK Square (402340.KS)**
SK Square is the investment holding company spun off from SK Telecom, holding major stakes in SK Hynix (000660.KS) and other SK-group tech ventures. The screen flags it for a striking combination: 84.1% operating margin (reflecting mark-to-market portfolio gains and dividend income), 37.8% ROE, near-zero debt (9% debt ratio), and trailing P/E of just 10x — historically cheap for a vehicle with Hynix embedded. RS at the 98.5th percentile confirms price momentum aligns with the fundamental quality case. With the Hynix AI memory cycle accelerating, the SK Square NAV discount trade is increasingly in focus.

**#2 — Onconics Therapeutics (476060.KQ)**
A clinical-stage oncology biotech posting +260.8% revenue growth year-on-year — the standout growth velocity in today's list. ROE of 24.7% and a low 5.1% debt ratio are unusual for a company this early in the commercialization curve, suggesting a licensing or milestone payment event may have dropped into the base period. Operating margin of 23.6% is notably positive for a biotech. The high trailing P/E (63x) prices in continued execution; this is a high-conviction/high-risk name.

**#3 — Bosung Powertech (006910.KS)**
A structural steel and pressure vessel manufacturer — directly exposed to the power infrastructure buildout theme that dominated today's market narrative (SMP +47%, ESS contract flow). Revenue growth of +91.3% YoY with 20.3% ROE, 18.2% operating margin, and a conservative 20.9% debt load. RS percentile of 95.8 confirms this is not a value trap — price is trending. At 25.2x trailing P/E, it screens as a reasonably priced compounder in a sector with genuine near-term catalysts. Watch for whether today's energy/power thematic translates into sustained institutional buying in names like this.
