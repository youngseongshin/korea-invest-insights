## Market Wrap

The April 10 Korea session — the most recent close briefing available — delivered a sharply bifurcated market. The headline read Risk-On, but the character underneath was **selective compression**: money flowed hard into a narrow set of leaders while the majority of the board struggled to hold intraday gains.

**Semiconductor and telecom infrastructure** carried the session. Samsung Electro-Mechanics (삼성전기, 009150.KS) and Samsung Electronics (삼성전자, 005930.KS) anchored the large-cap semiconductor bid, while the more dramatic action unfolded in RF and fiber names. RF Materials (RF머트리얼즈), Daehankwangtong (대한광통신), Solid (쏠리드, 098660.KQ), RFHIC (218410.KQ), and KMW (케이엠더블유, 032500.KQ) were cited repeatedly across real-time flow feeds, with RF Materials registering a +22.5% single-session surge. The common thread: AI infrastructure buildout requiring domestic RF components and fiber connectivity — not a one-day event, but a broadening of the AI hardware spend thesis downstream from chipmakers.

**Construction and Middle East reconstruction** generated strong news flow. Hana Securities issued a Buy with a ₩240,000 target on Hyundai Engineering & Construction (현대건설, 000720.KS), citing Q2 sentiment recovery and Iraq/Syria reconstruction exposure. NH Investment maintained a Positive sector view on construction. However, execution was messy — names like Daewoo Construction (대우건설, 047040.KS) failed to hold VWAP by close, underscoring the gap between positive analyst framing and actual price-action quality.

**Power/energy** was a structural talking point — SMP prices reportedly surged +47% — with ESS order pipeline expectations surfacing in flow commentary. But without direct-entry setups clearing, the sector remained an observation rather than an action item.

**Weak side:** Biotech underperformed broadly, with repeated flow signals calling for a biotech underweight relative to IT/semiconductor components. Pearl Abyss (펄어비스, 263750.KQ) was the notable laggard among large-cap names, with sustained institutional selling. The session's clear message: **concentration, not rotation**.

Foreign buying appeared focused on semiconductor large-caps, with Samsung Electronics sustaining foreign net buy momentum. Institutional activity was more mixed — confirmation of at least one flow leg (foreign *or* institutional) in semis was the bar set for the next session.

## Today's Screener Spotlight: Quiet Accumulation

*Wednesday's screener. Data from 2026-04-14.*

**Methodology:** The Quiet Accumulation screener targets stocks *before* a breakout, capturing names where foreign investors are steadily building positions during a price consolidation phase. Filters require a 30-day price range ≤24%, RSI between 38–62 (neutral momentum), close above the 60-day MA (uptrend intact), and a 20-day increase in foreign ownership of at least +0.05pp. This is the complement to momentum screeners — it hunts the coil, not the spring.
