*SK hynix scrapped the 1000% PS cap and tied the bonus pool to 10% of operating profit through last year's labor agreement. The February 2026 payout averaged roughly KRW 150M per employee. Samsung Electronics is now considering 12% of DS-segment operating profit as a special bonus. Combined, the two run at roughly KRW 62.5T per year — 2.35% of nominal GDP, 5.04% of household consumption. That's a supplementary-budget-scale private cash transfer. Some Korean media call it "the key to a consumption revival." The data say otherwise. Beneficiaries are \~110,000 people, marginal propensity to consume is low, and most of the cash flows into property and the stock market. The result is not "average-line recovery" — it is "accelerated K-shape." Real alpha is at the two ends of the K, and in the infrastructure between them.*

## Key takeaways

* **Bonus pool size (on 2026E consensus)**:
  - SK hynix: KRW 227.8T OP × 10% = **\~KRW 22.8T**
  - Samsung Electronics DS: KRW 331.3T OP × 12% = **\~KRW 39.8T**
  - **Combined \~KRW 62.5T** (2.35% of nominal GDP, 5.04% of household consumption)
* **Actual domestic-consumption impact**: base-case **\~KRW 7.6T**, or **0.28% of GDP**
  - Why: 42% effective tax, 25% post-tax marginal propensity to consume, 70% domestic share, 1.2x secondary multiplier
* **Beneficiary count**: \~110,000–120,000 across both companies (0.23% of the population)
* **Not "consumption recovery" — a "K-shaped reset"**:
  - **Asset effect**: property absorbs the cash (commuter-belt areas + Gangnam) → asset-price inflation → headwind for non-owners
  - **Consumption split**: premium +60%, value-tier stable, **middle market collapses**
  - **Geographic reset**: southern Gyeonggi + North Chungcheong become an "AI Capital Belt"
  - **Talent reallocation**: a new Korean acronym for prestige tracks now includes "semiconductor," changing student preference data
* **The real macro impact**: not the bonus (KRW 62.5T), but **tax revenue + capex + capital-market re-rating**
* **Four most actionable VC theses**:
  1. **Mass-affluent WealthTech** (50,000–150,000 new affluent households)
  2. **PropTech 2.0** (commuter-belt + HNWI real estate)
  3. **AI-infrastructure-adjacent SaaS** (power, cleanroom, test)
  4. **Legal / tax / estate automation** (5–50억 KRW household white space)
* **Single biggest risk**: AI capex cycle slowdown (2027–2028 possibility)

## 1. The size — what KRW 62.5T actually means

### 1.1 How the bonus pools build up

```
SK hynix:
- 2026E OP consensus:        KRW 227.8T
- Bonus pool ratio:           10% of OP
- Math:                       227.8T × 10% = 22.8T
- Per-employee (avg):         22.8T / \~35,000 = \~KRW 650M

Samsung Electronics (DS-segment, conditional on #1):
- 2026E DS OP consensus:      \~KRW 331.3T
- Special-bonus pool ratio:   12% of OP (under consideration)
- Math:                       331.3T × 12% = 39.8T
- Per-employee (avg):         39.8T / \~78,669 = \~KRW 510M

Total: 22.8T + 39.8T = 62.5T

Sanity check:
- Per-employee average \~KRW 510–650M (pre-tax)
- \~4x SK hynix's 2025 KRW 150M
- 7–10x the average Korean household income (\~KRW 68M)
```

### 1.2 Versus GDP and household consumption

```
Korea 2025 nominal GDP:          \~KRW 2,663.3T
Household consumption (46.6%):    \~KRW 1,241.1T

KRW 62.5T relative weight:
- vs nominal GDP:                 62.5 / 2,663.3 = 2.35%
- vs household consumption:       62.5 / 1,241.1 = 5.04%
- vs 2025 supplementary budget (KRW 31.8T): \~1.97x

Read:
- Supplementary-budget-scale private cash transfer
- A meaningful macro variable
- But "meaningful" ≠ "consumption recovery"
```

### 1.3 The amount that actually reaches domestic consumption is much smaller

```
Formula:
Domestic consumption impact = total bonus
                            × (1 − effective tax rate)
                            × post-tax MPC
                            × domestic-share
                            × secondary multiplier

Scenarios:

Bear:
62.5 × (1−45%) × 15% × 60% × 1.1 = \~KRW 3.4T
vs GDP: 0.13%

Base (most realistic):
62.5 × (1−42.5%) × 25% × 70% × 1.2 = \~KRW 7.6T
vs GDP: 0.28%

Bull:
62.5 × (1−40%) × 35% × 80% × 1.3 = \~KRW 13.7T
vs GDP: 0.51%

→ Most realistic estimate: KRW 7.6T
→ +0.61% on household consumption
→ Meaningful, but not a "recovery"
```

### 1.4 Why the marginal propensity to consume is low

```
A high-earner's typical use of an extra KRW 100M:

1. Taxes: \~42–45% (KRW 42–45M)
   → flows to the government

2. Debt paydown / saving: \~20–30% (KRW 15–30M)
   → not consumption — debt reduction

3. Asset investment: \~25–35% (KRW 25–35M)
   → flows into equities, property, bonds
   → "assets," not "consumption"

4. Actual consumption: \~10–25% (KRW 10–25M)
   - Of which premium consumption: \~70%
   - Of which general consumption: \~30%

5. Overseas spending / leakage: \~5–10%
   - Travel, imported luxury, foreign equities

Compare (lower-income):
- Taxes: \~10–15%
- Saving: \~5–10%
- Consumption: \~70–85%
→ MPC \~70–85%

→ The same KRW 100M in different hands produces
   3–5x different domestic-consumption impact.
```

## 2. What is actually changing — six axes of the K-shaped reset

### 2.1 Capital markets: KOSPI becomes an "AI memory ETF"

```
Structural change:

Past (2010s):
KOSPI = average of Korea's main industries
Samsung Electronics + SK hynix weight: \~25–30%

Today (2026):
Samsung Electronics + SK hynix weight: \~44%
Operating-profit share: >50%
Export contribution: \~20%

→ Korean capital markets converge into a single-theme AI / memory fund
→ Foreign capital does not view Korea as an "undervalued value market"
   but as "AI memory beta"
→ KOSPI volatility = NVIDIA / HBM cycle volatility

Implications:
- Limited capital-allocation room for non-semi industries
- Core-business weakness → talent loss → vicious cycle
- Non-AI traditional manufacturing and SMEs face harder conditions
```

### 2.2 Labor market: the new prestige ladder and two-way disconnect

```

Channel 2: SMEs / suppliers → giants

→ Internal talent stability at the giants and

## 3. Three macro variables bigger than the bonus

### 3.1 Variable 1 — fab capex (>KRW 1,000T cumulative over 10 years)

```
Bonus: \~KRW 62.5T per year.
Fab capex: >KRW 1,000T cumulative over 10 years.
→ \~16x larger.

This is the real macro variable.

First-order: fab construction itself
- Construction / EPC: \~KRW 200T
- Equipment / materials: trillions of KRW per year in orders
- Gas / chemicals / logistics

Second-order: infrastructure (\~50% co-funded by government)
- Power: transformers, cables, ESS, generation
- Water: water / sewerage, treatment
- Roads: industrial-park access, regional arteries
- Residential: housing for employees and contractors

Third-order: regional economy
- Local consumption (F&B, healthcare, education)
- Local real estate
- Local employment

Core read:
This capital flows into "capital accumulation," not "consumption."
→ Short-term consumption effect is smaller
→ But it restructures the long-term Korean economy.
```

### 3.2 Variable 2 — tax revenue + redistribution

```
Incremental tax revenue from the two firms:

Direct:
- Corporate tax: OP boom → \~KRW 100T+
- Income tax: bonus boom → \~KRW 25–31T
- VAT: consumption uplift → \~KRW 5–10T
- Combined: \~KRW 130–140T

What happens with it:

Scenario A: industrial / infrastructure re-investment
- Power-grid expansion
- AI / R&D support
- Regional infrastructure
- Effect: capital accumulation, long-term growth

Scenario B: redistribution
- Low-income transfers
- Youth / family support
- Regional consumption vouchers
- Effect: stronger short-term consumption uplift

Most likely mix: \~60% A + \~40% B
→ Both effects fire
→ Short-term consumption is bigger via B.

Implications:
- Bonus alone runs at MPC \~25%
- Redistribution to lower income runs at MPC \~70–85%
- Same money = 3–4x effect
→ "Indirect redistribution" is the stronger consumption channel.
```

### 3.3 Variable 3 — capital-market wealth effect

```
KOSPI impact:

Samsung Electronics: +125.6%
SK hynix:           +179.4%

- Direct equity-ownership households in Korea: \~30%

- Direct effect is therefore limited

But indirect channels are strong:
- NPS / corporate pensions assets up
- ETF / pension balances up
- Financial-asset valuations up
- Trading volume up → brokerage profits up

Kiwoom Securities 1Q26:
- OP: KRW 621.2B (+90.9%)
- Avg daily turnover: KRW 56.3T (March 4)

→ Bonus + KOSPI rally compound

→ More attractive than direct consumer-stock plays

Three wealth-effect channels:
1. Direct: equity owners → asset effect → consumption
2. Indirect: turnover up → brokerage profits → industry
3. Psychological: rising-asset mood → broader sentiment

## 5. Four misconceptions in the market

### 5.1 #1 — "The bonus = consumption recovery"

```
Wrong inference:
"KRW 62.5T = consumption revival"

Reality:
- Headline: KRW 62.5T
- Post-tax: \~KRW 36T
- Actual consumption: \~KRW 7.6T (base case)
- vs GDP: 0.28%

→ "Meaningful macro variable" and "consumption recovery" are not the same
→ Complement, not substitute.

What real recovery needs:
- Household-debt relief
- Property normalization
- SME / self-employed recovery
- Better job quality
- Fertility-rate recovery

→ KRW 62T of bonus cannot solve all of those.
```

### 5.2 #2 — "The beneficiaries will drive nationwide consumption"

```
The math:

Beneficiary population:
- SK hynix: \~35,000
- Samsung Electronics DS: \~78,669
- Combined: \~110,000–120,000

Korea total population: \~51.4M
Share: \~0.23%

Household level:
- Korea total households: \~22M
- Beneficiary households: \~110,000–120,000
- Share: \~0.5–0.6%

→ Less than 0.6% of all Korean households
→ Too narrow to drive national consumption on its own
→ But can drive specific categories (premium).
```

### 5.3 #3 — "Trickle-down will lift the rest of the economy"

```
Trickle-down theory:
"Higher upper-income incomes → more consumption → broader industry revenue → more jobs."

Korean evidence:
- IMF (2015) meta-analysis: trickle-down barely operates
- Korean housing wealth effect (+1% home prices → +0.1–0.2% consumption): weak
- Top-2% property wealth, MPC \~45% (low-income \~70%+)
- Conclusion: wealth growth at the top does not convert into consumption easily

This cycle shows the same pattern:
- Bonus → property and equities (assets)
- Some premium consumption
- Weak pass-through to general consumption

→ Redistribution is more effective than trickle-down
→ How the government deploys the additional tax base is decisive.
```

### 5.4 #4 — "This run lasts forever"

```
Durability check:

Bullish (extends into 2027–28):
- AI capex cycle persists
- Big-tech infra build-out
- HBM4 / HBM5 product cycle
- Inference-era memory demand

Bearish (decelerates in 2027–28):
- AI capex peaks
- Memory cycle tops
- Big-tech in-house silicon accelerates
- Macro slowdown

Probability sketch:
- Permanent: \~10% (structural change)
- Persists (2027–28): \~50%
- Decelerates: \~30%
- Sharp decline: \~10%

Most likely path:
2026–2027 strength persists; 2028 decelerates.

→ Don't bet on "forever"
→ A cycle-down hedge is mandatory
→ Avoid business models dependent on a single industry.
```

## 9. The one-line bottom line

The combined Samsung Electronics + SK hynix bonus pool of KRW 62.5T is a supplementary-budget-scale private cash transfer — 2.35% of nominal GDP, 5.04% of household consumption. That is a meaningful macro variable. **But "meaningful" does not mean "consumption recovery."** The base case for actual domestic-consumption impact is \~KRW 7.6T, or 0.28% of GDP. The reason: beneficiaries are \~110,000 people (0.23% of the population), the marginal propensity to consume runs at \~25%, and most of the cash is absorbed into real estate, equities, and overseas spending.

**The real change is not "average-line recovery" — it is "K-shaped reset."** Korea's capital markets converge into an "AI memory ETF" (44% of KOSPI), the labor market reorders into the new prestige acronym, real estate splits into "commuter-belt + Gangnam," consumption splits into luxury vs value-tier, and geography reshapes into "southern Gyeonggi + North Chungcheong AI Belt" vs the rest. The middle market disappears first.

**The bigger macro variables sit elsewhere.** The bonus (\~KRW 62.5T per year) is the tip of the iceberg. ① Fab capex (>KRW 1,000T cumulative over 10 years) is \~16x larger; ② if the additional tax revenue (\~KRW 130–140T per year) is redistributed, MPC rises 3–4x; ③ the capital-market wealth effect flows directly into brokerage turnover. **The bonus is a "consumption event"; capex, taxes, and capital markets are "structure events."**

**For VC and equity investors, real alpha lives at the two ends of the K and in the infrastructure between them.** Mass-affluent WealthTech (50,000–150,000 new affluent households), PropTech 2.0 (commuter belt), AI-infrastructure-adjacent SaaS, and legal / tax / estate automation are the four most actionable theses. On listed equities, the priority order is Kiwoom Securities (capital-market beta), fab-infra 2nd tier (Hanyang Eng, GST), Hyundai Department Store (premium consumption), then power infra on pullback.

**The single biggest risk is the inflection of the AI capex cycle.** Hedge with 10–15% in cycle-agnostic B2B SaaS and the lower end of the K (value-tier, subscription) against a 2027–2028 slowdown. **"Bet on the average and you lose. Bet on the two ends of the K and the infrastructure between them — that is the answer."** The difference will not determine a quarter's return — it will determine the 5-year compounded return.
